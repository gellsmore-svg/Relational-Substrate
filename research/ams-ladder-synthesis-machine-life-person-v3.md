# AMS Ladder Synthesis Machine Life Person v3

## Purpose

This note refreshes the lower ontological ladder after the ecology layer has now been canonically mapped.

The earlier `v2` ladder already stated the core progression:

```text
machine
-> life
-> person
```

and compressed it well as:

```text
Machines work, life recovers, persons answer.
```

That remains strong.

What now needs sharpening is the middle rung.

The ecology work has made it clear that life should no longer be stated only as:

- self-renewing bounded continuity;
- or recoverable machine-remaking coherence;

but more explicitly as:

```text
recoverable machine ecology under real burden
```

This file gives the ladder that final pass.

## Core Thesis

The current best ladder compression is:

```text
Machines perform ordered work under load, living systems are recoverable machine ecologies under real burden, and persons are embodied living subjects raised into inward continuity, moral agency, and meaningful addressability.
```

This is now the strongest current compact statement of the lower ladder.

## Why This Is Stronger Than v2

The earlier `v2` ladder was already strong in three ways:

- it gave each rung a clearer positive definition;
- it integrated burden into the machine and life layers;
- it strengthened the anti-reductionist distinctions.

What is stronger now is that the life rung is no longer supported only by:

- self-renewal;
- recoverability;
- burden-conditioned coherence.

It is now supported by the canonical ecology layer, including:

```text
A_burden
C_burden
R_cap
R_res
B_sync
C_sup
F_casc
V_E
θ_rec
```

That means the ladder now rests on a cleaner internal architecture.

## The Three Rungs

## 1. Machine

The strongest current machine line remains:

```text
A machine is an ordered system that performs bounded work through structured transitions and can remain coherent, compensate, drift, or fragment under load.
```

This remains correct and should stand.

The machine rung includes:

- ordered work;
- threshold behaviour;
- burden export;
- structured failure styles.

Machines can therefore be:

- complex;
- adaptive;
- symbolically productive;
- locally self-correcting;
- dangerous;

without thereby becoming living systems.

This remains the correct first rung.

## 2. Life

The strongest current life line is now:

```text
A living system is a bounded continuity that still owns a recoverable machine ecology under real burden.
```

This is now the decisive middle rung.

The life rung includes:

- bounded continuity;
- self-renewing machine ecology;
- owned recoverability;
- burden partitionability;
- reserve, support, and recovery-loop dominance.

That is materially better than simply saying:

- life is activity;
- life is complexity;
- life is generic self-maintenance.

Life now means:

```text
the ecology still helps itself back into coherence strongly enough to preserve a real future of self-renewing order
```

That is the strongest current middle claim in the corpus.

## 3. Person

The strongest current personhood line remains:

```text
Personhood is embodied living selfhood raised into inward continuity, moral agency, and meaningful addressability.
```

This remains the decisive upper rung of the lower ladder.

The person rung includes:

- embodied living continuity;
- inward continuity;
- responsible agency;
- meaningful moral and relational addressability.

That means personhood still cannot be reduced either to:

- machinery;
- biology alone;
- symbolic performance alone.

This remains the right upper boundary.

## The Canonical Ecology Layer Beneath Life

The most important new addition in `v3` is that the life rung should now explicitly rest on the canonical ecology layer.

The ecology layer's core variables are:

```text
A_burden = acute burden
C_burden = cumulative burden
R_cap    = recovery capacity
R_res    = reserve depth
B_sync   = burden synchronisation
C_sup    = cross-support strength
F_casc   = cascade susceptibility

```

These variables now tell us what the life rung means in more exact terms.

The strongest current ecology-based life criterion is:

```text
life remains while V_E stays above θ_rec strongly enough that owned ecological recoverability is still real
```

That should now be treated as the formal underside of the life rung.

That is a major gain since `v2`.

## Burden Across the Ladder

The ladder is stronger now because burden is treated differently at each rung.

## Machine Burden

Machines can:

- compensate;
- drift;
- fragment;
- export burden.

That is real, but not yet life.

## Living Burden

Living systems remain living while:

- reserve remains sufficient;
- support remains effective;
- synchrony remains dampable;
- recoverability remains owned.

This is the decisive middle distinction.

## Personal Burden

Persons remain persons through:

- impairment;
- blocked expression;
- burdened living continuity;
- reduced performance.

This is the decisive upper distinction.

Thus the ladder is not only a ladder of positive additions.

It is also a ladder of what remains true under burden and failure.

## Edge-Case Strength

The ladder is now stronger than before because it places the hard cases more cleanly.

## Complex Symbolic Systems

These remain on the machine rung because:

- they can be powerful;
- they can be adaptive;
- they can be symbolically impressive;

without being recoverable living ecologies.

## Dormancy and Severe Stress

These remain on the life rung because:

- activity may be sparse or burdened;
- but ecological recoverability remains owned.

## Hollow Support and Mixed Spiral States

These remain critically important life-layer states because:

- function may continue;
- compensation may continue;
- yet robust future recoverability may already be thinning.

This is one of the biggest gains since `v2`.

## Infancy, Sleep, Impairment, and Coma

These remain on the person rung because personhood belongs to:

- the continuing embodied subject;
- not merely to present maximal performance.

The lower ladder now supports this more cleanly than before.
