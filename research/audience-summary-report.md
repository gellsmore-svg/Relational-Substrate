# AMS v2.0 Audience Review Programme Summary

Date: 2026-03-11
Status: summary report

## Audience Agent Definitions

Created agent specs:
- `ams-review-gcse-uk`
- `ams-review-alevel-uk`
- `ams-review-bachelors`
- `ams-review-phd`

These act as specialist reviewers, not rival authors.

## Key Findings From Online Homework

1. GCSE readers need short conceptual steps, concrete-first teaching, and frequent recap.
2. A-level readers can handle real abstraction, but still need explicit transitions and layer-tagging.
3. Bachelor’s readers tolerate higher abstraction if argument structure and term discipline remain visible.
4. PhD/professional readers demand status precision, clean hierarchy, and explicit separation of ontology from mechanism speculation.

## Comparative Differences

Main differentiators:
- axiom load
- abstraction tolerance
- need for examples
- recap frequency
- theological explanation density

## Recommended Master-Book Shape

Recommended architecture:
- one unified master book
- a 25-chapter drafting spine for the master manuscript
- strong early chapters explicitly carrying the nexology, ontology, theology, and philosophy messages
- expanded electricity section
- integrated companion and non-technical material
- explicit speculative mechanism layer

## Suitable Chapter Count Range

Recommended master-manuscript target:
- 25 chapters

Acceptable master-manuscript range:
- 22 to 28 chapters

Audience-edition rule:
- GCSE and some A-level editions may need more chapters and shorter chapter length
- bachelor’s editions will likely stay close to the master count
- PhD/professional editions may preserve or sometimes compress the count

## Major Risks By Audience

### GCSE
- early ontology overload
- hidden assumptions
- theological shorthand becoming opaque

### A-level
- insufficient transition control
- drifting between layers without warning

### Bachelor's
- hidden dependency chains
- status confusion in mechanism chapters

### PhD / Professional
- rhetorical inflation
- insufficient objection handling
- unresolved mechanism treated as settled
