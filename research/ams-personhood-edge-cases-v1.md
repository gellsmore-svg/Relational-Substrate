# AMS Personhood Edge Cases v1

## Purpose

This note pressure-tests the personhood boundary at the edges.

The previous personhood file argued:

```text
Personhood is not mere life and not mere symbolic activity. It is embodied living selfhood raised into inward continuity, moral agency, and meaningful addressability.
```

That statement is strong in ideal cases.

The present question is:

```text
does it remain stable when visible agency, cognition, or responsiveness are reduced?
```

This file exists to harden the boundary rather than leave it persuasive only for clear adult normative cases.

## Central Claim

The central claim is:

> Personhood is not measured by the current intensity of manifest rational performance or visible agency. It is measured by the continuity of the embodied personal subject who remains the kind of being capable of inward selfhood, moral addressability, and covenantal relation, even when the present exercise of those capacities is undeveloped, impaired, dormant, obscured, or externally unexpressed.

## Why Edge Cases Matter

If personhood is tied too tightly to:

- current self-report;
- current rational fluency;
- current linguistic output;
- current decision-making performance;

then personhood becomes unstable precisely where moral seriousness is often greatest.

A sound ontology must cope with:

- infancy;
- sleep;
- coma;
- severe cognitive impairment;
- dementia;
- temporary incapacity;
- hidden but unexpressed subject continuity.

Without this, the machine/life/person ladder becomes brittle.

## The Core Distinction

The key distinction is:

```text
current manifest agency
≠
personhood status
```

A person may not currently be:

- speaking;
- choosing visibly;
- reflecting explicitly;
- reasoning discursively;

and yet remain the same personal subject.

The personhood boundary therefore must be framed in terms of:

```text
continuity of the personal subject
```

rather than:

```text
momentary performance level
```

## Edge-Case Categories

The current ontology supports five major personhood edge-case classes.

## 1. Infancy and Early Development

### Problem

Infants do not yet display mature rational agency, moral articulation, or explicit self-interpretation.

If personhood required the current full exercise of those capacities, infants would be placed in an unstable category.

### AMS reading

Infancy should not be treated as:

```text
pre-personal material waiting to become someone later
```

but as:

```text
the same kind of embodied personal subject in an early and undeveloped exercise state
```

The decisive point is:

```text
personhood belongs to the subject whose capacities are developing,
not only to the mature display of those capacities
```

### Conclusion

Infancy is not a counterexample to personhood. It is a case of:

```text
personal continuity under immature expression
```

## 2. Sleep and Temporary Inactivity

### Problem

During sleep, visible agency, explicit rational control, and active self-report are reduced or absent.

If personhood depended on uninterrupted active manifestation, sleep would create absurd discontinuity.

### AMS reading

Sleep preserves:

- embodied continuity;
- identity continuity;
- recoverable agency;
- continued moral and relational addressability of the same subject.

The person is therefore not lost in sleep. The person is:

```text
present under altered exercise mode
```

### Conclusion

Sleep shows clearly that personhood is not identical to current active self-expression.

## 3. Coma and Deep Unresponsiveness

### Problem

Coma or deep unresponsive states are much harder because manifest agency may be absent for prolonged periods.

### AMS reading

The decisive issue is not:

```text
is the person currently speaking or choosing visibly?
```

but:

```text
does the same embodied personal subject remain in continuity,
even if expression is profoundly blocked?
```

As long as the personal subject remains in continuity with the living embodied self, absence of manifest output alone does not erase personhood.

### Important caution

This does not trivialise severe cases.

It means only:

```text
blocked expression is not the same thing as absent subject
```

### Conclusion

Coma presses the distinction between:

- expression;
- existence of the subject.

The AMS account should preserve that distinction.

## 4. Severe Cognitive Impairment and Dementia

### Problem

Severe impairment may reduce:

- memory;
- rational fluency;
- social recognition;
- behavioural control;
- explicit self-interpretation.

If personhood were indexed to current high-level cognitive performance, it would become unstable precisely where continuity of care and moral seriousness most matter.

### AMS reading

Severe impairment should be read as:

```text
damage or obscuration in the exercise of personhood,
not automatic erasure of the person
```

This fits the broader corruption grammar:

```text
continuity remains while powers become fragmented, obscured, or misdirected
```

The personal subject remains the bearer of that damage.

### Conclusion

Impairment is not non-personhood.
It is often:

```text
personhood under damaged or obscured exercise
```

## 5. Residual Biological Life Without Recoverable Personal Expression

### Problem

Some edge cases may involve continued biological life while the integrity of higher-order personal exercise appears profoundly broken.

These are the hardest cases because they test whether life and personhood can come apart.

### AMS reading

The ontology must be careful here.

It should not collapse immediately into:

```text
biological life = full manifest personhood in the same way as before
```

but neither should it collapse into:

```text
reduced expression = no person
```

The safer first-pass claim is:

```text
where embodied living continuity of the same human subject remains,
personhood should be presumed continuous even when higher-order expression is severely occluded
```

This keeps the ontology:

- continuity-protective;
- non-reductionist;
- morally serious.

### Conclusion

This is a zone for careful restraint, but the current AMS direction should lean toward continuity of the person unless true identity break is established rather than merely reduced expression observed.

## Refined Personhood Criteria

The edge-case analysis suggests a refined test.

A person should be understood as:

### 1. An Embodied Living Subject

Not a detached ghostly entity and not mere symbolic process.

### 2. The Same Subject Through Variable Expression

Personhood persists through:

- immaturity;
- sleep;
- impairment;
- blocked communication;
- partial loss of manifest control.

### 3. A Being Ordered to Moral and Relational Address

Even when that address cannot presently be articulated back at full clarity.

### 4. A Continuity That Is Damaged Rather Than Nullified by Obscuration

This is the key edge-case principle.

## Compact Boundary Refinement

The best current refinement is:

```text
Personhood belongs to the continuing embodied subject, not only to the present maximal exercise of personal powers.
```

Expanded:

```text
The person remains the same morally and relationally addressable embodied subject across development, sleep, impairment, and blocked expression, so long as continuity of the subject remains and has not been replaced by a merely different entity or by non-personal machine activity.
```

This is materially stronger than tying personhood to current visible performance.

## Comparison with AI

This refinement also sharpens the AI boundary.

An AI system may display:

- language;
- planning;
- simulated self-reference;
- high symbolic fluency.

But it does not thereby gain personhood.

Why?

Because personhood here is not being defined by:

```text
present visible symbolic sophistication
```

but by:

```text
continuing embodied personal subjecthood and moral addressability
```

That keeps the ontology from drifting into performance-based inflation.

## Comparison with Life Boundary

The life-edge file argued:

```text
a system remains living while its bounded identity still owns a recoverable machine-remaking loop
```

The personhood edge refinement now adds:

```text
personhood remains while the same embodied subject continues,
even where personal powers are immature, latent, impaired, or blocked in expression
```

This keeps the ladder stable:

- machine;
- life;
- person.

## Remaining Tensions

This file improves the boundary, but some tensions remain.

Open questions include:

- exact treatment of developmental beginnings;
- exact treatment of irreversible catastrophic impairment;
- how to distinguish subject occlusion from true subject break without overclaiming;
- how best to relate moral addressability to non-manifest agency in severe cases.

These require later work.
