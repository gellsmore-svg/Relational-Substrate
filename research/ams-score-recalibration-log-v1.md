# AMS Score Recalibration Log v1

## Purpose

This log records confidence-score changes across the AMS substrate coherence programme.

The scores are meant to be comparative and revisable. This log exists to prevent silent drift.

Every recalibration entry should say:

- what score changed
- why it changed
- what comparison forced the change
- whether the change is likely stable or still provisional

## Scoring Principle

The `1-10` confidence score does not measure truth in the abstract.

It measures:

- how well constrained and comparatively defensible an AMS interpretation currently is within the active corpus

The score should rise when:

- a case scales coherently into higher-order analysis
- the case survives comparison with neighbouring cases
- the AMS interpretation explains both persistence and failure better than thinner alternatives

The score should fall when:

- a case becomes more underconstrained in comparison
- scaling introduces explanatory rupture
- the AMS language appears to rename rather than explain

## Recalibration Round 1

### Trigger

The first `T1C` stress-test corpus produced enough higher-order comparison to revisit some earlier `T1B` scores.

The main trigger cases were:

- water networks
- quartz
- glass
- metallic iron crystal / grain structures
- magnetite with domain relevance

### Round 1 Updates

#### 1. `H2O`

- `Old score`: `7/10`
- `New score`: `8/10`
- `Reason`:
  - the `T1C` water-network analysis strengthened the claim that the local grouped case scales coherently into a real higher-order whole
  - the AMS reading now has stronger comparative support than it had in the purely local `T1B` pass
- `Stability`: provisional but likely durable

#### 2. `SiO2`

- `Old score`: `7/10`
- `New score`: `8/10`
- `Reason`:
  - quartz and glass both strengthened the claim that repeated `SiO2` grouped closures can scale into real higher-order wholes
  - the case now has support across both crystal and non-crystal higher-order regimes
- `Stability`: provisional but strong

#### 3. Metallic `Fe`

- `Old score`: `5/10`
- `New score`: `6/10`
- `Reason`:
  - the T1C iron-grain analysis showed real scaling into higher-order material identity
  - however, the T1B-to-T1C boundary remains conceptually difficult, so the rise should stay modest
- `Stability`: provisional

#### 4. `Fe3O4`

- `Old score`: `6/10`
- `New score`: `7/10`
- `Reason`:
  - magnetite domain relevance provided a stronger path from grouped oxide chemistry into whole-level ordered behaviour
  - the AMS interpretation gained support through scaling rather than through local description alone
- `Stability`: provisional but promising

## No-Change Notes

Some scores were intentionally not changed in Round 1.

### `O3`

- `Score retained`: `8/10`
- `Reason`:
  - the T1C corpus did not materially improve or weaken the metastability reading
  - the case remains strong at the T1B level as a metastable-only benchmark

### `FeCl3`

- `Score retained`: `4/10`
- `Reason`:
  - no later corpus material yet constrained the coordination-sensitive AMS mapping strongly enough to justify revision

### `C6H6`

- `Score retained`: `6/10`
- `Reason`:
  - the T1C corpus did not yet provide the larger aromatic or polymer-based comparative pressure needed to recalibrate ring-closure confidence

## Round 1 Summary

The first recalibration round supports two general lessons:

1. `T1C` scaling can materially improve `T1B` confidence where higher-order whole behaviour reinforces the local grouped interpretation.
2. Confidence should rise slowly, not dramatically, when the T1B-to-T1C boundary remains conceptually hard.
