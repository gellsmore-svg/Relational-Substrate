## Source Anchors

The source base supports these facts:

- Phospholipids are amphipathic molecules with hydrophilic head groups and hydrophobic hydrocarbon tails.
- Phospholipids spontaneously form bilayers in water, with hydrophobic tails buried inside and polar heads facing aqueous compartments.
- Lipid bilayers form stable barriers between aqueous compartments and are the basic structure of biological membranes.
- Membranes behave as two-dimensional fluids where lipids and proteins can move laterally.
- Membrane fluidity depends on temperature and lipid composition.
- Shorter fatty acid chains reduce packing strength and increase fluidity.
- Cis-double bonds introduce kinks in hydrocarbon chains, making them harder to pack and helping membranes remain fluid at lower temperatures.
- Cells can adjust fatty acid composition to maintain membrane fluidity.
- Cholesterol inserts into bilayers and regulates membrane structure, thickness, permeability, and fluidity.
- Cholesterol can order adjacent acyl chains and alter bilayer packing.
- Lipid oxidation can alter membrane properties, cause permeabilization, and contribute to cell death.
- Membranes with different degrees of unsaturation respond differently to oxidative damage.
