# AMS Respiratory Chain and ATP Synthase Integrated Route Map v1

## Purpose

This note integrates two systems that were previously treated mainly in bridged form:

- the respiratory electron-transport chain;
- ATP synthase.

The locked family identities are:

```text
Carbon carries molecular architecture.
Nitrogen carries biological availability and recognition.
Phosphorus carries transferable biological order.
Sulfur carries controllable chemical reactivity.
Iron carries redox depth.
Oxygen carries polarity and oxidative power.
```

The question here is:

```text
can the six-family grammar explain one of biology's deepest runtime machines as a single coherent route?
```

## Central Claim

The central claim is:

> The respiratory chain and ATP synthase together form the clearest current example of the AMS runtime grammar in action: iron carries staged redox depth, sulfur closes and governs parts of that depth, oxygen completes it as terminal sink, oxygen-water fields carry proton asymmetry, phosphorus captures the resulting ordered state in ATP, and carbon-nitrogen protein architecture embeds the whole machine into durable readable form.
