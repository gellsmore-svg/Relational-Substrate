## Source-Supported Working Facts

The source literature supports these working facts:

```text
Sulfur is critical in living cells.
Sulfur is incorporated into proteins as cysteine and methionine.
Sulfur participates in iron-sulfur clusters, cofactors, vitamins, and RNA modifications.
Iron-sulfur clusters are ancient, ubiquitous, and fundamental biological cofactors.
Common Fe-S clusters include [2Fe-2S], [3Fe-4S], and [4Fe-4S].
Fe-S clusters mediate electron transfer in respiration, photosynthesis, and metabolic pathways.
Fe-S clusters also support catalysis, sensing, gene regulation, DNA replication/repair, ribosome biogenesis, nucleotide metabolism, and non-redox enzyme roles.
Fe-S clusters are typically coordinated by cysteine residues, though other residues can participate.
Fe-S cluster assembly requires organised cellular biogenesis machinery such as ISC, NIF, and SUF systems.
Fe-S clusters are sensitive to oxidation and small molecules such as oxygen and nitric oxide.
Cysteine can participate in spontaneous Fe-S cluster formation under some prebiotic-relevant conditions.
```
