# Coherent Biblical Ontology English Master v1 Derivative Policy

## Purpose

This file defines the derivative policy for all later editions that descend from the English master.

The purpose of this policy is to ensure that:

- later versions remain recognisably derived from the master;
- simplification does not become distortion;
- appendix growth does not re-enter the core spine by drift;
- educational and translation editions inherit the same argument rather than rebuilding it independently;
- future work proceeds under clear governance.

The purpose is not to create those later editions yet.

It is:

```text
to define the rules by which all later versions must descend from the English master
```

## Central Claim

The central claim is:

> The English master is now stable enough to govern all later editions. Future bachelor, A-level, GCSE, translation, appendix-bearing, or companion versions must therefore be produced by controlled derivation from the master rather than by fresh reconstruction from research files. Derivative work may simplify, pace differently, explain more openly, or package material differently, but it must preserve the master's chapter order, governing ontology, and main distinctions unless it is explicitly declared a separate companion work rather than a derivative edition.

More briefly:

```text
Later versions may simplify the book, but they may not reinvent it.
```

## Canonical Source Rule

The canonical source for all ordinary derivative editions is:

```text
coherent-biblical-ontology-english-master-v1.md
```

No ordinary derivative edition should derive directly from:

- research corpus files;
- packet files;
- assembly-plan files;
- review-control files;
- previous lower-level derivatives.

The chain must be:

```text
English master -> derivative edition
```

not:

```text
research archive -> derivative edition
```

## What All Derivative Editions Must Preserve

All true derivative editions must preserve the following.

## 1. Chapter Order

The following order is mandatory:

1. Created Order
2. Material Grammar
3. Machine Order
4. Life as Recoverable Ecology
5. Personhood
6. Corruption
7. Redemption
8. Resurrection Continuity

Derivative editions may retitle chapters for audience fit, but they must not reorder the argument.

## 2. Governing Thesis

All derivatives must preserve the governing claim that reality is:

```text
created bounded continuity
```

and that its elemental, machine, living, personal, and redemptive orders are real and coherent.

## 3. Lower Ladder

All derivatives must preserve, in recognisable form, the ladder:

```text
Machines work, life restores, persons answer.
```

This may be expanded or simplified for audience level, but not replaced with a different ontology.

## 4. Corruption Logic

All derivatives must preserve corruption as:

```text
parasitic fragmentation of real continuity
```

and must not drift into:

- corruption as illusion;
- corruption as coequal first principle;
- corruption as mere absence of function.

## 5. Redemption Logic

All derivatives must preserve redemption as:

```text
restorative reordering of the same wounded continuity
```

and must not let redemption collapse into:

- escape;
- replacement;
- mere declarative relabelling.

## 6. Resurrection Logic

All derivatives must preserve resurrection as:

```text
restored embodied continuity after real rupture
```

and must not reduce it to:

- survival of a detachable self alone;
- replacement by a merely similar being;
- symbolic continuation only.

## What Derivative Editions May Simplify

Derivative editions may simplify several things.

## 1. Sentence-Level Difficulty

They may use:

- shorter sentences;
- more explicit transitions;
- less compressed abstraction;
- more visible orientation language.

## 2. Paragraph Density

They may:

- break paragraphs more often;
- slow the middle chapters down;
- add breathing room around the hardest distinctions.

## 3. Support Language

They may:

- explain key claims more patiently;
- restate the middle-ladder transitions more openly;
- use more didactic guidance where the master assumes more reader maturity.

## 4. Vocabulary Load

They may:

- reduce technical vocabulary;
- replace some higher-order phrasing with simpler equivalents;
- carry a bounded glossary where necessary.

## 5. Example Policy

They may:

- add one or two more audience-fitting illustrations;
- make analogies more explicit;
- use more concrete phrasing so long as it does not distort the claim.

## What Derivative Editions Must Not Simplify Away

Derivative editions must not simplify away:

- the primacy of order;
- the six-family material grammar in recognisable form;
- the reality of machine order;
- life as more than activity;
- personhood as more than function or language;
- corruption as parasitic rather than primary;
- redemption as restoration rather than substitution;
- resurrection as continuity rather than bypass.

If those go missing, the derivative is no longer faithful to the master.

## Audience-Level Derivative Guidance

Different derivative levels should simplify in different ways.

## Bachelor-Level Derivative

The bachelor version should:

- preserve most of the conceptual structure;
- reduce compression;
- unpack middle chapters more clearly;
- retain strong ontology vocabulary with more support.

## A-Level Derivative

The A-level version should:

- preserve the chapter climb;
- simplify the ontology vocabulary more aggressively;
- make transitions and distinctions more explicit;
- use cleaner didactic pacing.

## GCSE Derivative

The GCSE version should:

- preserve the order and arc;
- use simpler syntax and more explicit framing;
- reduce density sharply;
- keep the strongest contrasts in very plain language.

## Translation Derivatives

Translation derivatives should:

- preserve the master structure first;
- preserve the governing vocabulary second;
- adapt phrasing to target-language dignity and intelligibility third.

Translation should not become freer simply because simplification is possible.

## Appendix Policy

The master now implies a clear appendix boundary.

The following materials are appendix-eligible or companion-text eligible rather than core-spine material:

- AI ontology material
- extended vorton formalism
- heavy mathematical scaffolding
- corpus-control material
- research score and calibration material
- extensive stress-test technical detail

These materials may be published, but they should not drift back into the eight-chapter master unless the whole publishing aim changes.

## Companion-Work Rule

If a future text:

- changes the chapter order;
- moves AI back into the core argument;
- foregrounds formal substrate mathematics;
- or significantly enlarges the book beyond its current scope,

then it should be treated as:

```text
a companion work
```

not as a derivative edition of the English master.

## The Best Current Derivation Rule

The strongest current derivation rule is:

```

That is the main governance formula for all downstream work.

## The Best Current Risk Warning

The main downstream risk is:

```

That is what this policy is meant to prevent.
