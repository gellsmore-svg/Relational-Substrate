## Source Anchors

The source base supports these facts:

- Atmospheric dinitrogen is abundant but chemically inert because of the strong N-N triple bond.
- Nitrogenase is the enzyme system responsible for biological conversion of N2 into ammonia.
- Mo-nitrogenase is the most studied nitrogenase isoform; V- and Fe-only nitrogenases also exist.
- Mo-nitrogenase consists of Fe protein and MoFe protein components.
- The Fe protein contains a [4Fe-4S] cluster and supplies electrons to the MoFe protein in an ATP-dependent cycle.
- The MoFe protein contains P-clusters and FeMo-cofactor, with FeMo-cofactor acting as the catalytic site for N2 reduction.
- Nitrogenase catalysis requires coordinated proton, electron, and ATP coupling.
- The commonly represented stoichiometry includes N2 reduction to ammonia with obligatory H2 evolution and about 16 ATP per N2 reduced.
- Nitrogenase electron transfer is conformationally gated and involves transient association/dissociation of Fe protein and MoFe protein.
- Nitrogenase is oxygen-sensitive, requiring biological protection strategies such as heterocysts, respiratory protection, physical barriers, root nodules, leghemoglobin, or conformational protection.
- Biological nitrogen fixation remains a major source of bioavailable nitrogen on Earth alongside industrial Haber-Bosch ammonia synthesis.
- Haber-Bosch achieves ammonia synthesis industrially at high temperature and pressure, whereas nitrogenase operates under physiological conditions but with high ATP demand and oxygen-management constraints.

## Route Map 6: Oxygen Sensitivity and Protection

Nitrogenase is highly oxygen-sensitive.

AMS route tension:

```text
oxygen supports respiration and ATP supply.
oxygen damages nitrogenase and redox clusters.
```

This is not a minor operational issue. It is a core route conflict. Nitrogen fixation needs energy and reductant, but the machinery that performs it can be damaged by oxygen.

Protection strategies include:

- heterocysts in cyanobacteria;
- temporal separation of photosynthesis and fixation;
- respiratory protection;
- root nodules and microaerobic environments;
- leghemoglobin oxygen buffering;
- biofilms and diffusion barriers;
- conformational protection proteins.

Vorton interpretation:

```text
oxygen-protection vorton = boundary field that permits energy while excluding destructive oxygen access
```

Route profile:

```text
route class: R4/R6
obstruction class: O4/O6
dominance: D4
grade: G_A/G_B
```

AMS principle:

```text
an element can be required for the support route and destructive to the target route.
```

Oxygen helps make ATP but threatens nitrogenase.
