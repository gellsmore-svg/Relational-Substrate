# Audience Profile: PhD-Level Readers and Professionals

Date: 2026-03-11
Status: research baseline for AMS v2.0 audience review

## Purpose

This document defines the likely reading profile for advanced critical readers: postgraduate students, early doctoral readers, and professionals reading AMS at its most rigorous public level.

## Research Basis

Online reference points used:
- graduate/scientific writing guidance emphasising clarity, audience control, concise structure, and explicit argument flow
- Nature Masterclasses and comparable scientific-writing guidance focused on structure, titles, abstracts, and writing for specialists and non-specialists
- higher-level philosophy-writing guidance stressing thesis precision, premise control, and objection handling

Research takeaway:
- this readership accepts high abstraction
- but it is least tolerant of vagueness, redundancy, status drift, and rhetorical inflation
- structural precision matters more than friendliness
- epistemic status labelling is essential

## Reader Baseline

Likely safe assumptions:
- strong tolerance for abstraction
- familiarity with formal argument and disciplinary prose
- ability to track layered distinctions across long chapters
- expectation that terminology will be stable and defensible

Unsafe assumptions:
- background agreement with AMS theology
- willingness to forgive ambiguous ontology
- patience for motivational framing

## Sentence Structure

Recommended style:
- medium to long sentences are acceptable
- high clause density is acceptable if sharply controlled
- definitions can be compact
- prose may be concise, but must not become cryptic

Tolerance profile:
- nested reasoning: high
- abstraction: high
- inferential compression: high if signposted

## Vocabulary Profile

This audience can absorb specialized vocabulary rapidly.

What matters most is not simplification but:
- consistency
- precision
- scoped definition
- non-drifting usage

## Explanatory Method

Best fit:
- architecture first
- then argument
- then application and limitation

This audience expects:
- explicit claim status
- direct engagement with objections
- clear separation of ontology, mechanism, theology, and empirical aspiration

## Assumption Load

This audience can track a high premise load, but still demands visible hierarchy.

Reasonable load:
- 5 or more active premises in a chapter
- multiple interacting distinctions

Failure point:
- when the prose hides which claims are baseline, provisional, or exploratory

## Axiom Consumption Capacity

Recommended ceiling:
- 5 fresh premises in a chapter if the dependency order is explicit
- more is possible only with strict subsectioning

Recommended handling:
- axioms can be stated formally
- but each cluster should be followed by inferential clarification
- speculative claims must be labelled as such
- doctrinal ceilings should be stated as ceilings, not as disguised mechanism claims

## Conceptual Pacing

Recommended pace:
- fastest of the four audiences
- minimal recap
- strong forward motion

Useful supports:
- front-loaded architecture statements
- claim-status markers
- cross-reference pointers rather than repeated restatement

## Tone

Target tone:
- rigorous
- analytically calm
- precise
- economical
- non-performative

Avoid:
- polemical grandstanding
- faux certainty
- verbal excess

## Structural Expectations

Best fit:
- substantial chapters
- dense but clean subsectioning
- formal distinction maps
- appendices for speculative mechanism clusters
- explicit notes on what is and is not claimed

## Theological Handling

Advanced readers need:
- doctrinal boundaries stated cleanly
- rationale for those boundaries
- careful distinction between theological authority and physical mechanism conjecture

Failure mode:
- if theology is used as a slogan or as a shortcut around explanation

## Reader Fatigue Risk

Likely fatigue points:
- overlong apologetic detours
- repeated definitions without new payoff
- unresolved mechanism language treated as stable ontology

## Common Failure Modes

1. Confusing rigor with density.
2. Treating compression as a substitute for structure.
3. Failing to isolate speculative mechanism.
4. Overstating local explanatory gains.

## Writing Guidance For This Audience

The future PhD/professional version should:
- tighten aggressively
- formalize architecture
- isolate speculative sections clearly
- state open problems directly

## Recommended Master-Manuscript Implications

The master book should preserve:
- status-tagged claims
- conceptual hierarchy
- formal internal definitions
- appendicial or boxed speculative mechanism sections
- objection-ready structure
