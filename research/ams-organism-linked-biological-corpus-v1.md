# AMS Organism-Linked Biological Corpus v1

## Purpose

This corpus deepens the biological side of the AMS programme beyond generic higher-order wholeness.

The earlier corpora already established growing strength around:

- grouped micro-organisation
- higher-order wholes
- boundaries and interfaces
- defect significance
- retained history
- teleology and function

This document now asks a sharper question:

- can AMS carry organism-linked biological organisation without splitting into separate ontologies for chemistry, structure, regulation, and function?

The target is not “biology in general.” The target is the region where:

- molecular organisation
- boundary maintenance
- regulation
- repair
- developmental order
- energy conversion

all belong to one living whole.

## Governing Question

The main question is:

- when does a biological case show not just higher-order wholeness, but organism-linked organisation?

An organism-linked case should normally show several of these together:

- role within a larger living whole
- regulation rather than mere persistence
- repair or quality control
- strong failure significance when organised role is lost
- boundary-sensitive or environment-sensitive viability

## Source Discipline

This corpus is grounded at the conventional level by standard cell and molecular biology sources, especially:

- NCBI Bookshelf cell biology texts
- OpenStax biology
- primary or review literature on chaperones and proteostasis

The AMS interpretation remains an ontological mapping, not a claim that the biological sources themselves teach AMS.

## Case 1: Chaperone-Mediated Protein Folding

### Why This Case Matters

Protein folding is already a strong biological assembly case. Chaperone-mediated folding deepens it because it adds:

- regulated assistance
- quality control
- repair-like rescue
- fate decisions between folding and degradation

### Observed

Chaperone systems:

- assist folding
- prevent or reverse misfolding
- support assembly
- help determine whether a protein is repaired or degraded

### AMS interpretation

This is a major organism-linked case because it shows that biological wholeness includes active preservation of viable form, not mere passive persistence.

- `Whole type`: regulated organism-linked assembly support
- `Boundary significance`: moderate
- `Repair significance`: very high
- `Teleological strength`: `Strong`
- `Failure mode`: aggregation, misfolding, degradation, loss of organised function
- `Higher-order significance`: very high

### Confidence

- `Observed`: strong
- `Strong AMS inference`: strong
- `Confidence score`: `8/10`

These cases show that organism-linked wholes are marked not only by persistence, but by:

- regulation

- function-bearing

The biological cases make it harder to treat real identity as only static retention. Organism-linked identity is often:

- dynamic
- regulated
- history-sensitive
- role-sensitive
