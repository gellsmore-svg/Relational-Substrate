## What Has Changed

The addition of `I4` changes the iron family in three important ways.

### 1. The Family Is More Continuous

Previously the iron family was strongest at:

- heme biology;
- Fe-S biology;
- governance and toxicity;
- mineral and geological roles.

But the catalytic middle outside heme was underdeveloped.

Now the family more clearly spans:

```text
heme chamber
-> non-heme catalytic depth
-> Fe-S relay depth
-> storage/governance
-> toxicity boundary
-> mineral and geomagnetic expression
```

That is a better continuity arc.

### 2. The Identity Sentence Has Been Stress-Tested in a New Geometry

The most important question was:

```text
is iron really the redox-depth family,
or was that overfitted to heme and Fe-S systems?
```

The non-heme catalytic file strongly supports the existing identity.

Outside heme, iron still appears as:

- oxygen-activating depth;
- high-valent catalytic depth;
- governed difficult oxidation;
- dangerous escape if uncoupled.

That is exactly the iron family logic.

### 3. The Old Reason for the Score Cap Has Weakened

The previous synthesis was explicit that one reason for not moving higher was:

```text
missing I4 detail
```

That reason no longer stands.

## What Still Limits the Family

Even after the new file, some limiting factors remain.

### 1. Geological Breadth Is Still the Loosest Zone

`I8` remains valuable but less constrained than the best biochemical submodels.

The issue is not that it is weak. The issue is that:

- timescales are long;
- route evidence is less tightly bounded;
- historical interpretations remain more approximate.

### 2. Family Breadth Still Requires Governance Language

Iron is not a simple one-mode family.

It appears as:

- gas handler;
- electron relay;
- catalytic depth centre;
- storage problem;
- toxicity problem;
- magnetic mineral;
- geochemical memory.

The family remains coherent only because the governance condition is strong:

```text
iron depth must be embedded, stored, transported, governed, or mineralised
```

That breadth still argues for some caution in scoring.

### 3. Full Formal Calibration Is Still Missing

The new formal scaffold helps, but iron is not yet quantitatively calibrated across:

- heme versus non-heme depth;
- Fe-S relay versus catalytic branch choice;
- storage/governance thresholds;
- ferroptotic boundary mathematics.

So the family is stronger, but not fully formalised.

## Score Decision

The recalibration question is whether iron should now move above `9/10`.

### Argument for staying at `9/10`

- The family was already strong.
- Geological and broad-context roles remain the loosest part.
- Formal calibration is not complete.
- Breadth and governance dependence still cap overconfidence.

### Argument for moving upward

- The clearest missing biochemical role has now been filled.
- The central identity survives a major new geometry.
- The family now has strong direct support across chambered, open, relay, governance, failure, and mineral forms.

### Best current judgment

The best current score is:

```text
9.25/10 provisional upward revision
```

This is the most defensible move.

Why not remain at `9/10`:

The previous explicit cap reason has materially weakened, and the family now deserves a modest upward adjustment.

Why not move to `9.5/10`:

The geological end and the full formal calibration still lag behind the strongest biochemical submodels.
