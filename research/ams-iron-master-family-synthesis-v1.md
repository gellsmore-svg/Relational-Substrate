## Core Family Mechanism

Iron repeatedly appears where a system needs deep electron-state capacity:

- oxygen binding;
- oxygen reduction;
- electron transfer;
- substrate oxidation;
- peroxide handling;
- DNA and metabolic enzyme chemistry;
- Fe-S redox relay;
- cellular iron allocation;
- redox failure and lipid peroxidation;
- magnetic mineral identity;
- geological redox memory.

Iron is powerful because it can change redox state in useful biological ranges.

Iron is dangerous for the same reason.

AMS formula:

```text
Fe2+/Fe3+ cycling
+ coordination flexibility
+ heme embedding
+ sulfur-cluster closure
+ storage/transport governance
+ mineral phase capacity
= redox depth
```

The governing rule:

```text
iron must be embedded, stored, transported, governed, or mineralised to remain useful.
```
