# AMS Priority Research Roadmap v1

## Purpose

This note converts the current AMS pressure points into a practical research order.

The goal is not to list everything that could be done. It is to decide:

```text
what should be done first
what depends on what
what most increases programme strength
```

The previous pressure-point file established that AMS is strongest where it is:

- comparative;
- mechanism-facing;
- scale-disciplined;

and weakest where:

- formalisation is thin;
- vorton geometry is imprecise;
- edge cases remain under-tested;
- high-level synthesis outruns lower-level stress.

This roadmap turns that into a sequence.

## Central Principle

The central principle is:

```text
Prioritise work that increases constraint before work that increases scope.
```

That means:

- formalise before extending;
- harden before multiplying;
- test edge cases before making broader claims;
- improve dependency-critical layers first.

## Priority Order

The recommended research order is:

```text
1. Substrate and vorton formalisation
2. Element-family edge-case hardening
3. Molecule-to-machine quantitative bridge
4. Life/person boundary edge-case analysis
5. Arc-level synthesis hardening
6. Controlled extension into broader domains
```

## Priority 1: Substrate and Vorton Formalisation

### Why first

This is still the single largest technical weakness.

Most of the programme now depends on:

- route grammar;
- closure language;
- vorton identity;
- topology-sensitive persistence.

Those ideas are powerful, but still less mathematically constrained than they need to be.

### Immediate objectives

- define candidate vorton classes more rigorously;
- state closure conditions more formally;
- define transition criteria between route topologies;
- tie variables such as `B_in`, `B_out`, `Q_coh`, `J_bound`, `J_trans`, `D`, `I_comp` into clearer mathematical or semi-formal relations;
- compare AMS route classes against known field, condensed-matter, network, or dynamical-systems analogues without collapsing them into those frameworks.

### Deliverable target

Best next technical artifacts in this lane:

```text
ams-vorton-geometry-and-closure-formalism-v1.md
ams-route-variable-mathematical-framework-v1.md
ams-topology-transition-criteria-v1.md
```

### Why it matters

Without this layer hardening, the rest of the programme remains strongest as ontology and weaker as constrained modelling.

## Priority 2: Element-Family Edge-Case Hardening

### Why second

The family identities are among the strongest assets in the whole programme.

That makes them worth protecting.

The goal is not to invent new families yet, but to stress-test the existing locked ones where they are most vulnerable.

### Main targets

- sulfur in context-dependent sulfate and mineral-history cases;
- iron in non-heme catalytic roles and borderline governance cases;
- oxygen in planetary-scale and atmospheric-history transitions;
- carbon where architecture meets failure or extreme topology;
- phosphorus where transfer order becomes pathologically mislocated or excessively multiplexed.

### Deliverable target

Best next artifacts in this lane:

```text
ams-iron-non-heme-catalytic-route-map-v1.md
ams-oxygen-planetary-scale-source-sink-stress-test-v1.md
ams-sulfur-sulfation-pattern-specificity-stress-test-v1.md
ams-phosphorus-pathological-mislocation-and-calcification-v1.md
```

### Why it matters

If the compressed family identities survive edge cases, the programme becomes much harder to dismiss as elegant but loose.

## Priority 3: Molecule-to-Machine Quantitative Bridge

### Why third

The programme already has:

- strong family files;
- representative molecule bridge;
- machine-scale synthesis.

What it still lacks is tighter constraint at the bridge between them.

### Main targets

- score representative molecules and complexes more rigorously;
- define what counts as a strong versus weak family fit in a concrete system;
- make machine-scale route models more explicit about dependency chains and failure propagation;
- develop a clearer relation between molecule-level topology and system-level coherence.

### Deliverable target

Best next artifacts in this lane:

```text
ams-ribosome-route-dependency-matrix-v1.md
ams-respiration-failure-propagation-matrix-v1.md
ams-core-runtime-machines-quantitative-comparison-v1.md
```

### Why it matters

This is where the ontology shows that it can do more than narrate systems. It can discriminate stronger and weaker fits.

## Priority 4: Life and Personhood Edge Cases

### Why fourth

The machine/life/person ladder is one of the clearest recent gains, but it remains vulnerable at the margins.

That kind of vulnerability matters because the higher-level ontology can collapse quickly if edge cases are mishandled.

### Main targets

- dormant life;
- extremely simple life;
- damaged or impaired living systems;
- infancy;
- coma and severe cognitive impairment;
- continuity of personhood when manifest agency is reduced.

### Deliverable target

Best next artifacts in this lane:

```text
ams-life-boundary-edge-cases-v1.md
ams-personhood-edge-cases-v1.md
ams-identity-continuity-under-impairment-v1.md
```

### Why it matters

This is where the higher-order ontology becomes either humane and stable or brittle and exclusionary.

## Priority 5: Arc-Level Synthesis Hardening

### Why fifth

The creation-corruption-redemption synthesis is now coherent, but it is still one of the broadest and most synthetic layers.

It needs to be brought under the same pressure discipline as the stronger lower-level files.

### Main targets

- compare multiple corruption cases more explicitly across layers;
- make redemption claims more structurally comparative rather than only verbally compressed;
- strengthen the continuity argument for restoration and resurrection with additional counter-model comparisons;
- distinguish more sharply between restoration, replacement, escape, and counterfeit continuation.

### Deliverable target

Best next artifacts in this lane:

```text
ams-corruption-case-comparison-matrix-v1.md
ams-restoration-versus-replacement-comparison-v1.md
ams-resurrection-continuity-stress-test-v1.md
```

### Why it matters

This is how the top of the ontology becomes as disciplined as the bottom.

## Priority 6: Controlled Extension

### Why sixth

Only after the earlier priorities are strengthened should the programme extend aggressively into:

- social systems;
- ecclesial order;
- civilisational fragmentation;
- governance theory;
- explicit eschatological synthesis.

### Why delayed

These domains are high-risk because they can easily become rhetorically rich but mechanically under-constrained.

The lower layers must therefore be hardened first.

### Deliverable target


## Dependency Logic

The roadmap is not arbitrary. It has a dependency chain.

### Dependency Chain

```text
formal substrate work
-> stronger family identities
-> stronger molecule/machine constraints
-> stronger life/person boundaries
-> stronger corruption/redemption arc
-> safer high-level extension
```

This is the right direction because each level depends on the one beneath it being stable enough.

## Fastest High-Value Wins

If only a few tasks can be done next, the highest-value set is:

### 1. `ams-vorton-geometry-and-closure-formalism-v1.md`

Because it addresses the biggest technical weakness directly.

### 2. `ams-iron-non-heme-catalytic-route-map-v1.md`

Because iron is already strong and this closes one of the most obvious family gaps.

### 3. `ams-life-boundary-edge-cases-v1.md`

Because the life/personhood ladder is strategically important and needs hardening early.

### 4. `ams-corruption-case-comparison-matrix-v1.md`

Because it would make the arc-level work more comparative and less purely synthetic.

## Work to Avoid Right Now

To keep the programme disciplined, avoid prioritising:

- broad new element families before the core is hardened;
- social or civilisational extrapolations before boundary and arc work are stabilised;
- additional high-level synthesis files that do not add new constraint;
- stylistic reformulations of already strong claims instead of technical strengthening.

## Compact Roadmap Grammar

The best current compression is:

```text
First formalise the substrate, then harden the families, then tighten the molecule-to-machine bridge, then pressure-test life and personhood boundaries, then discipline the arc, and only after that extend the programme outward.
```
