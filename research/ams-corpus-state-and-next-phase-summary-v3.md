# AMS Corpus State and Next-Phase Summary v3

## Purpose

This note refreshes the programme-control summary after the recent ecology and life-boundary deepening phase.

The earlier `v2` summary already reflected:

- the strengthened machine layer;
- the refreshed top thesis;
- the cleaner family core;
- the iron lock review.

Since then, the corpus has advanced materially again through:

- weighted machine-export work;
- acute versus cumulative burden modelling;
- dual-metric cell-state thresholds;
- recovery ecology modelling;
- synchronisation, support, and reserve formalisation;
- mixed spiral and hollow-support severe-state analysis;
- a much stronger `life boundary synthesis v3`.

This file updates the programme-control summary so it reflects that new state.

## Central Claim

The central claim is:

> The AMS corpus now has a much stronger ecology of living continuity than before: the machine layer is no longer only threshold-bearing, but feeds into a coupled recovery-ecology model in which reserve, support, synchronisation, and cascade determine whether burden remains recoverable or becomes death-approach. The next phase should therefore focus less on adding more categories and more on deciding where to consolidate, where to formalise further, and where the strongest current corpus should now be reused upward.

More briefly:

```text
The corpus is no longer mainly missing a life model; it is deciding how far to formalise and consolidate the one it now has.
```

The corpus no longer treats severe decline as one generic collapse bucket.

This is a major gain in precision.

One of the strongest current contributions of the ecology layer is that it now explains why:

- activity can continue;
- repair can continue;
- compensation can continue;

while:


This is a major conceptual gain.

## 5. The Top and Bottom of the Ontology Are Better Joined

The strongest current corpus is now better joined from:

- substrate and family roles;
- through machines;
- through life and personhood;
- into corruption, redemption, and resurrection.

The joins are still not finished, but they are much less loose than before.

## What Remains Weakest

Even after the ecology phase, several weaknesses remain.

## 1. The Formal Ecology Layer Is Still Semi-Formal

The ecology layer is much stronger than before, but it remains:

- banded;
- comparative;
- equation-shaped rather than experimentally anchored;
- dependent on analyst-chosen coefficients.

That is acceptable for now, but still a real limit.

## 2. The Corpus Now Risks Fragmentation by Success

This is a new kind of weakness.

The technical ecology work is now strong enough that it can fragment into too many local files if not carefully consolidated.

This means the next phase should be selective.

## 3. Programme Reuse Is Behind the New Ecology Layer

The life synthesis and top thesis have improved, but there are still likely summary and synthesis layers that do not yet fully reflect:

- hollow support;
- mixed spirals;
- ecology-level recoverability;
- loop-based death approach.

This is now a consolidation problem more than a discovery problem.

## 4. Experimental or Quantitative Anchoring Is Still Distant

The corpus is now much more structured, but it is still not experimentally anchored in the strong scientific sense.

That is not a surprise, but it remains true.

## Next-Phase Priorities

The next phase should focus on three things.

## 1. Consolidation of the Ecology Layer

Highest-value targets:

- fold the ecology model into the main reusable summaries;
- reduce duplication across closely related severe-state files;
- decide which ecology files are canonical and which are exploratory.

This is probably the single best immediate task class.

## 2. Selective Formal Refinement

The strongest current variables for further deepening are:

- synchronisation;
- support;
- reserve;
- loop dominance.

But further work should now be selective rather than expansive.

## 3. Upward Reuse

The stronger life and ecology model should now be reused upward in:

- top-level summaries;
- boundary summaries;
- arc summaries;
- any book-facing synthesis derived from the research corpus.

## What Not to Do Next

The corpus is now large enough that weak next moves are costly.

Avoid prioritising:

- broad new domains before the ecology layer is consolidated;
- more new severe-state categories unless they add real discrimination;
- false precision in the formal ecology layer;
- repeated high-level restatement with no integration value.
