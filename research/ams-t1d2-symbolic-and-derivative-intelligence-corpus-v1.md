# AMS T1D2 Symbolic and Derivative Intelligence Corpus v1

## Purpose

This corpus extends the AMS research programme into `T1D2`:

- creature-constructed relational order

The earlier corpora are now strong on:

- material grouped persistence
- higher-order wholes
- boundaries and interfaces
- defects, hysteresis, and retained history
- teleology and organism-linked biological regulation

This document now asks whether the same ontology can also carry:

- language
- symbolic systems
- software systems
- institutions
- markets and governance forms
- derivative intelligence

without treating them as:

- unreal fictions
- ontologically primary realities
- or disconnected metaphysical layers

## Governing Question

The main question is:

- when does a creature-constructed relational order become a real whole with genuine explanatory force, while still remaining derivative rather than ontologically primary?

AMS should answer that `T1D2` systems are:

- real
- higher-order
- often function-bearing
- dependent on prior creaturely realities

That means they must be described neither as illusions nor as self-grounding ultimate structures.

## Key Rule

This corpus must preserve the difference between:

- `intrinsic created relational order` (`T1D1`)
- `creature-constructed relational order` (`T1D2`)

Language, software, and institutions can be very real. But they remain derivative.

They depend on:

- embodied creatures
- prior intelligibility
- shared reality
- social and material support

## T1D2 Strength Criteria

A `T1D2` case is stronger when it shows most of the following:

- stable symbolic or relational structure
- recognisable boundary or membership conditions
- role-bearing persistence through time
- dependence on shared meaning or rule-governed coordination
- whole-level effects not reducible to one participant alone

## Case 1: Natural Language

### Why This Case Matters

Natural language is the clearest symbolic-system case in the programme.

It is neither merely private mental noise nor a brute physical object. It is a shared symbolic order sustained by embodied speakers within a real world.

### Observed

Natural language shows:

- shared symbolic structure
- grammar
- meaning-bearing communication
- durability through time
- role in naming, memory, coordination, and instruction

### AMS interpretation

Language is a real higher-order symbolic whole, but it is derivative from a prior intelligible reality.

It does not create the world it speaks about. It depends on:

- shared creaturely life
- a world already ordered enough to be named
- communities that preserve and transmit symbolic order

- `Whole type`: shared symbolic order
- `Boundary significance`: moderate to high
- `Persistence profile`: strong
- `Memory significance`: very high
- `Teleological strength`: `Strong`
- `Derivative status`: very strong
- `Failure mode`: semantic drift, fragmentation, incoherence, manipulation, breakdown of shared meaning

### Confidence

- `Observed`: strong
