# AMS Score Recalibration Log v2

## Purpose

This document records the second formal recalibration round for the AMS substrate coherence programme.

Round 1 was driven by the first `T1C` set:

- water networks
- quartz
- glass
- metallic iron crystal / grain structures
- magnetite with domain relevance

Round 2 is driven by the extended `T1C` set:

- polymers
- membranes
- alloys
- catalytic surfaces
- biological macromolecular assemblies

## Round 2 Principle

This round does not exist to push scores upward by default.

It exists to test whether the extended `T1C` cases:

- strengthen earlier `T1B` interpretations
- leave them unchanged
- expose underconstraint that should lower them later

The burden of proof remains conservative.

## Round 2 Updates

### 1. `C2H4`

- `Old score`: `6/10`
- `New score`: `7/10`
- `Reason`:
  - the polymer cases strengthened the idea that reactive but stable carbon grouped structures can scale coherently into higher-order wholes
  - `C2H4` now looks more like a genuine bridge case than a merely local reactive curiosity
- `Stability`: provisional but fairly strong

### 2. `C6H6`

- `Old score`: `6/10`
- `New score`: `7/10`
- `Reason`:
  - the expanded higher-order cases increased confidence that distributed ring closure is not an isolated elegant local pattern, but part of a wider family of stable higher-order carbon organisation
  - this does not solve every delocalisation issue, but it improves comparative defensibility
- `Stability`: provisional

### 3. Metallic `Fe`

- `Old score`: `6/10`
- `New score`: `7/10`
- `Reason`:
  - the alloy cases strengthened the claim that metallic local relation patterns do scale into genuine higher-order whole identity
  - the T1B-to-T1C boundary remains difficult, but less weakly supported than before
- `Stability`: provisional but improving

### 4. `FeCl3`

- `Old score`: `4/10`
- `New score`: `5/10`
- `Reason`:
  - catalytic surface analysis did not fully solve the coordination-sensitive mapping
  - but it did provide stronger boundary-centred comparison than existed in Round 1
  - the case remains underconstrained, but less isolated than before
- `Stability`: tentative

## Round 2 Summary

The second recalibration round suggests three broader lessons:

1. Carbon bridge cases are gaining comparative support faster than expected.
2. Boundary-defined and function-bearing wholes make context-sensitive chemistry more intelligible, but not yet strongly enough to justify aggressive score rises.
3. Metallic and mixed-metal higher-order cases are becoming more robustly defensible within AMS than they were after Round 1.
