# Audience Comparison Matrix for AMS v2.0

Date: 2026-03-11
Status: synthesis of audience research

## Purpose

This matrix compares the four target audiences so the master AMS v2.0 manuscript can be designed once and adapted later without conceptual betrayal.

## Comparative Matrix

| Dimension | GCSE UK | A-level UK | Bachelor's | PhD / Professional |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Sentence length | Short to medium | Medium | Medium to long | Medium to long |
| Clause density | Low | Moderate | Moderate to high | High if controlled |
| Vocabulary tolerance | Low jargon | Moderate jargon | Technical vocabulary acceptable | Specialized vocabulary acceptable |
| Abstraction tolerance | Low | Moderate | High | Very high |
| Example dependence | Very high | High | Moderate | Selective |
| Recap frequency | Very frequent | Frequent | Occasional | Minimal |
| Glossary need | High | Medium-high | Medium | Low-medium |
| Premise load per chapter | 2 new + 1 recalled | 3 new + 2 recalled | 4 new + subordinate layering | 5 new if hierarchy explicit |
| Best chapter size | Short / heavily subsectioned | Medium | Medium-long | Long / dense but ordered |
| Preferred explanation flow | Concrete to abstract | Definition plus example | Argument plus application | Architecture plus argument |
| Theology handling | Plain-language, explicit | Explicit and level-tagged | Formal and level-tagged | Strictly delimited, rationale stated |
| Main risk | Overload and discouragement | Layer confusion | Hidden dependency chains | Status drift and redundancy |

## Simplification Boundaries

### Necessary Simplification

- GCSE: more examples, more recap, less compression
- A-level: slower transitions and stronger definitions

### Harmful Simplification

- replacing AMS-specific terms with mainstream approximations
- removing theological guardrails instead of explaining them
- collapsing ontology and mechanism into one blurred layer

### Beneficial Expansion

- extra bridge sections
- chapter-purpose framing
- glossary support
- recap-ready endings

### Excessive Compression

- stacking multiple framework distinctions in one paragraph
- assuming readers infer claim status on their own
- leaving mechanism speculation untyped

## Axiom Capacity Comparison

| Audience | Safe New Axiom Load | Required Support |
|---|---|---|
| GCSE | 2 | narration, example, recap |
| A-level | 3 | explicit transitions, restatement |
| Bachelor's | 4 | visible hierarchy and subsectioning |
| PhD / Professional | 5 | status tagging and inferential architecture |

## Structural Implication For The Master Book

The master manuscript should be written above A-level and slightly below PhD compression by default:
- explicit enough to adapt downward
- precise enough to adapt upward
- structurally rich enough to split or compress later

## Master-Book Rule

If the master manuscript omits bridge paragraphs, claim-status markers, or recap-ready endings, later adaptation to GCSE and A-level will become inefficient and distortion-prone.

If the master manuscript omits formal structure, status discipline, and explicit hierarchy, later adaptation to bachelor’s and PhD audiences will become bloated and imprecise.
