## Source-Supported Working Facts

The source literature supports these working facts:

```text
DNA and RNA are nucleic acids with sugar-phosphate backbones.
The backbone consists of alternating sugar and phosphate groups.
Nucleotides are linked by phosphodiester bonds.
A phosphate group links the 5' carbon of one sugar to the 3' hydroxyl of the next sugar.
This creates 5' to 3' directionality.
DNA and RNA synthesis proceed by extension at the 3' hydroxyl.
DNA and RNA sequences are conventionally written 5' to 3'.
The sugar-phosphate backbone is negatively charged and hydrophilic.
In DNA, two sugar-phosphate backbones run antiparallel in the double helix.
Bases carry sequence specificity and pair inside the helix.
DNA is more chemically stable than RNA partly because DNA lacks the ribose 2' hydroxyl group.
RNA's 2' hydroxyl makes it more susceptible to hydrolysis and therefore more chemically labile.
```
