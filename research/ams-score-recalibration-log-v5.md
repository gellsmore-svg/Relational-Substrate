# AMS Score Recalibration Log v5

## Purpose

This document records the fifth formal recalibration round for the AMS substrate coherence programme.

Earlier recalibration rounds were driven by:

- `T1B` grouped micro-organisation
- first `T1C` whole formation
- boundary and interface pressure
- defect, hysteresis, and retained-history pressure
- teleology and function-bearing whole formation

Round 5 is driven by:

- organism-linked biological organisation
- regulation
- repair
- developmental order

This is a major pressure increase, because these cases test whether AMS can carry living organisation as one ontology rather than as a stack of loosely connected metaphors.

## Round 5 Principle

A score should rise in this round only where the current corpus now supports all or most of the following:

- higher-order whole identity
- strong boundary or interface significance
- organised regulation
- repair or quality-control relevance
- functional role inseparable from what the whole is

Scores should not rise merely because a case belongs to biology. Biological location alone is not evidence of better AMS constraint.

## Round 5 Summary

Round 5 produces four main conclusions:

1. The programme now has a real organism-linked benchmark layer rather than a generic “biology” layer.
2. Biological assemblies now look stronger because they are supported not just by persistence, but by regulation and quality-control logic.
3. Membranes and organismal regulation remain among the strongest overall cases in the entire AMS corpus.
4. The strongest living cases are now beginning to rival the strongest material cases in comparative confidence.
