# AMS Route Reality Benchmark Scores v1

## Purpose

This document applies the route-reality criteria from:

- `ams-route-reality-criteria-v1.md`

to the main benchmark cases in the programme.

The goal is to operationalise the route-reality framework by assigning provisional scores across the five criteria and then assigning a route-reality grade:

- `A`
- `B`
- `C`
- `D`

for the dominant route forms in each benchmark case.

## Scoring Method

Each benchmark route form is scored from `1-5` on each criterion:

1. Forward continuity
2. Directional commitment
3. Completion viability under progression
4. Dominance over local looping
5. Completion remains a live outcome

Interpretation:

- `1` = very weak
- `2` = weak
- `3` = mixed or conditional
- `4` = strong
- `5` = very strong

These are not truth scores. They are operational modelling scores.

## Grade Guide

- `A`
  - strong route reality across the criteria
- `B`
  - weak but real route reality
- `C`
  - apparent but unreliable route
- `D`
  - pseudo-route or non-robust route form

## Strongest Cross-Benchmark Findings

## 1. The Route-Reality Criteria Are Operational Enough to Score

This is the main result.

The criteria do not collapse under real cases. They can now be used in a disciplined way.

## 2. The Benchmark Grades Make Good Sense

The current grades align with the modelling so far:

- `A`
  - air -> glass viable handoff
  - crystal -> air viable ordered release
  - inward-biased route in polished metal, if treated in its own direction

- `B`
  - fragile but still real routes

- `C`
  - blocked apparent routes

- `D`
  - pseudo-routes and route-like activity without true completion logic

## 3. Glass Is the Best Mixed-Grade Test Bed

This is a major gain.

Glass cases carry:

- `B`
- `C`
- `D`

under closely related conditions.

That makes glass the strongest current material for frontier testing.

## 4. Crystal Preserves Route Reality Better

This is one of the clearest comparative results.

Even when crystal release fails, it more often fails as:

- a weak real route

than as:

- a pseudo-route

That is important and should be preserved in later modelling.

## 5. Direction Matters Deeply

The polished-metal case confirms:

- route reality cannot be treated as neutral with respect to direction

That is a major stabilising insight for the whole route framework.
