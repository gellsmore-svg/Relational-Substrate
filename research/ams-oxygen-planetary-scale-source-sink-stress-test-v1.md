# AMS Oxygen Planetary-Scale Source-Sink Stress Test v1

## Purpose

This note pressure-tests the broadest and most context-sensitive part of the oxygen family:

```text
planetary-scale source-sink competition
```

The oxygen family is currently locked at:

```text
Oxygen carries polarity and oxidative power.
```

That identity is very strong at:

- water-field behaviour;
- organic functional-group polarity;
- oxoanion charge perimeters;
- terminal respiration;
- ROS boundary chemistry.

One of the strongest remaining edge questions is:

```text
does the family remain disciplined when oxygen is treated not only as local chemistry,
but as atmospheric reservoir, geologic threshold, and planetary redox regime?
```

This file addresses that directly.

## Central Claim

The central claim is:

> Planetary oxygenation does not break the oxygen family identity. It stretches it to its widest scale. At planetary scale, oxygen still appears as polarity and oxidative power, but now in reservoir form: produced by water-splitting biology, consumed by respiration and oxidation of reductants, stabilised or destabilised by atmospheric and geologic feedbacks, and expressed as a source-sink field whose behaviour determines whether oxygen remains local and transient or becomes a world-shaping oxidant regime.

## Source Anchors

The source base supports these facts:

- Atmospheric O2 accumulates only when biological and geochemical sources exceed sinks over long timescales.
- The Great Oxidation Event (GOE) marks the first sustained rise of atmospheric oxygen, but the transition may have been unstable or oscillatory rather than smooth.
- Low atmospheric O2 states can be unstable under perturbations in source and sink fluxes.
- Oxygenation involves both biological production, especially oxygenic photosynthesis and organic-carbon burial, and the weakening or saturation of geochemical sinks.
- Phosphorus availability, oxidative metabolisms, burial efficiency, and mineral interactions can all influence oxygen source-sink balance.
- Atmospheric oxygen history therefore depends on coupled biospheric, geochemical, and photochemical feedbacks rather than a single simple source term.

## Why This Is a Real Stress Test

This is a harder case than local oxygen chemistry because:

- timescales are long;
- source and sink terms are multiple;
- atmospheric and geologic feedbacks are indirect;
- "oxygen" can easily become a loose historical label rather than a disciplined family term.

The pressure question is:

```text
is oxygen still doing one family-level thing here,
or has the family identity become too abstract to constrain the analysis?
```

This file argues the family still holds, but with more caution than at local scales.

## AMS Source-Sink Principle

The source-sink principle is:

```text
oxygen becomes a planetary regime only when its reservoir-form oxidative power is produced and retained faster than it is consumed by sinks
```

This means that oxygen's planetary role is not simply:

```text
oxygen is present
```

but:

```text
oxygen persists as an atmospheric oxidant field
because source-sink competition has crossed or maintained the right threshold
```

AMS interpretation:

```text
planetary oxygen is a thresholded oxidant field, not merely a gas concentration
```

## Family Identity Stress Test

The hard question is:

```text
should the oxygen family identity change because the planetary case is so broad?
```

### Arguments for changing it

- planetary oxygen is highly coupled to non-oxygen systems;
- the behaviour is source-sink historical rather than direct local chemistry;
- oxygen risks becoming a loose geohistorical label.

### Arguments for keeping it

- the central thing still at stake is persistence of oxidative power as a real reservoir;
- oxygen remains the decisive oxidant field whose presence or absence changes planetary regime;
- local oxygen chemistry and global oxygen reservoir are still one family at different scale;
- the source-sink language is a disciplined way of describing whether oxygen's oxidative power persists globally.

### Best judgment

The short identity should remain:

```text
Oxygen carries polarity and oxidative power.
```

But the planetary gloss should be sharpened:

```text
At planetary scale, oxygen's oxidative power appears as a thresholded atmospheric reservoir whose persistence depends on coupled source-sink competition.
```

This keeps the family intact while acknowledging the broader scale and looser constraints.
