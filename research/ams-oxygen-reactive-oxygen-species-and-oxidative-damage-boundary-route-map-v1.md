# AMS Oxygen Reactive Oxygen Species and Oxidative Damage Boundary Route Map v1

## Purpose

This note maps oxygen's `O6` role:

```text
O6: reactive oxygen species and oxidative damage boundary carrier
```

The oxygen family entry proposed:

```text
Oxygen carries polarity and oxidative power.
```

This file tests the fifth mechanism-facing claim: oxidative damage is not a separate chemistry from productive oxygen use, but the fragmented or misgoverned boundary of oxygen's terminal redox power, appearing as superoxide, peroxide, radical propagation, lipid peroxidation, protein oxidation, nucleic-acid damage, and system-level failure when antioxidant correction no longer keeps pace.

The central claim is:

> Reactive oxygen species are the topology of incomplete or misrouted oxygen closure. Productive respiration reduces oxygen to water under control; oxidative damage begins when that reduction fragments into partially reduced, diffusible, or chain-propagating states that attack surrounding biological order.

## Source Anchors

The source base supports these facts:

- Reactive oxygen species include superoxide, hydrogen peroxide, hydroxyl-radical-associated chemistry, and lipid peroxides.
- Mitochondrial respiration is a major endogenous source of ROS, especially through electron leak from respiratory complexes.
- ROS can act in signalling at low or controlled levels, but excessive ROS damages lipids, proteins, DNA, and RNA.
- Superoxide dismutases convert superoxide into hydrogen peroxide; catalase, glutathione peroxidases, peroxiredoxins, thioredoxins, and glutathione systems reduce peroxides and help maintain redox control.
- Lipid peroxidation is a major mechanism of membrane injury and is central to ferroptosis.
- Iron can accelerate damaging oxygen chemistry through radical-generating and lipid-peroxide-propagating pathways.
- Oxidative DNA damage includes lesions such as 8-oxoG/8-oxodG, while proteins can undergo thiol oxidation, methionine oxidation, carbonylation, nitration, and loss of function or aggregation.

Oxygen role:

```text
O6 = reactive oxygen species and oxidative damage boundary carrier
```

Associated roles:

Core distinction:

```text
productive oxygen closure = O2 -> H2O under gated routing
fragmented oxygen closure = partially reduced oxygen states escaping local control
```

This distinction is the entire point of `O6`. Oxygen damage is not "oxygen exists" but "oxygen closure failed to remain coherent."

## AMS Oxidative-Boundary Principle

```text
oxidative damage begins when oxygen power outruns route governance.
```

This can happen through:

```text
electron leak
```

That matters because superoxide, hydrogen peroxide, hydroxyl-radical-adjacent chemistry, and lipid peroxides do not behave identically. They occupy different positions on the route-fragmentation ladder.
