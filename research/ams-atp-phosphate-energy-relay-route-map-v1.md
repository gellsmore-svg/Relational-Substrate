## Source-Supported Working Facts

The source literature supports these working facts:

```text
ATP consists of adenine, ribose, and a chain of three phosphate groups.
The phosphate groups are conventionally named alpha, beta, and gamma.
The phosphate-phosphate linkages are phosphoanhydride bonds.
ATP hydrolysis to ADP and inorganic phosphate is exergonic.
ATP regeneration from ADP and inorganic phosphate requires energy input.
ATP often drives cellular work by reaction coupling.
ATP can transfer a phosphate group to another molecule, creating a phosphorylated intermediate.
Kinases catalyze phosphoryl group transfer from ATP to acceptor molecules.
ATP is not the strongest possible phosphoryl donor but occupies an intermediate position that lets it receive and donate phosphoryl groups.
ATP coupling often works through enzyme-mediated alternative reaction paths and intermediate states, not by a magical release of generic energy into the cell.
```

