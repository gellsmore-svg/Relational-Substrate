# AMS Ribosome and Translation Integrated Route Map v1

## Purpose

This note integrates the ribosome and the wider translation process as a single runtime machine.

The locked family identities are:

```text
Carbon carries molecular architecture.
Nitrogen carries biological availability and recognition.
Phosphorus carries transferable biological order.
Sulfur carries controllable chemical reactivity.
Iron carries redox depth.
Oxygen carries polarity and oxidative power.
```

The question here is:

```text
can the AMS core grammar explain the machine that converts readable sequence into protein architecture?
```

## Central Claim

The central claim is:

> Translation is the clearest current information-to-architecture machine in the AMS programme: nitrogen carries codon and anticodon recognition, phosphorus and oxygen carry the charged durable RNA backbone that makes this readability stable, carbon supplies the amino-acid and protein architecture that is ultimately built, phosphorus also carries the ATP/GTP-linked transferable order that powers charging and cycle progression, and oxygen-conditioned aqueous fields make the whole system readable, hydrated, and route-selective rather than collapsing into inert matter.
