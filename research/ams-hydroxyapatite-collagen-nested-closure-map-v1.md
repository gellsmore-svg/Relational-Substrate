## Source-Supported Mechanism Summary

The source literature supports the following working picture:

```text
osteoblasts secrete matrix vesicles
matrix vesicles accumulate Ca2+ and PO4^3-
calcium phosphate crystals nucleate and grow inside vesicles
crystals penetrate or exit the vesicle boundary
mineralized nodules attach to collagen fibrils
collagen fibrils mineralize from contact sites
bone mineral density increases during secondary mineralization
osteocytes regulate later mineralization and ion transport
```

Additional source-supported structural points:

```text
collagen fibrils provide a scaffold and oriented nanoscale structure
hydroxyapatite crystals are located within and around collagen fibrils
hydroxyapatite crystals can align with the collagen fibril axis
mineral content increases stiffness and load-bearing capacity
collagen contributes deformation response and toughness
hydroxyapatite bears high stress relative to collagen
```
