# AMS Experiment Log v1

## Purpose

This file is the first operational log for the AMS vorton experimental modelling programme.

Its role is simple:

- record modelling attempts
- state what behaviour was being targeted
- record what family or family-combination was used
- note what looked promising, what failed, and what needs remodelling

This log is not meant to contain final theory. It is the memory of the experimentation process.

## Logging Rules

Each entry should contain:

- `Experiment ID`
- `Date`
- `Target behaviour`
- `Material or system class`
- `Model family or family-combination`
- `Initial parameter intuition`
- `Expected outcome`
- `Observed modelling judgment`
- `Status`
  - coherent
  - promising
  - underconstrained
  - degenerate
  - remodel needed
- `Next move`

## Status Meanings

- `coherent`
  - the model family currently gives a plausible structured account with no immediate contradiction
- `promising`
  - the family gives useful explanatory traction but still needs better parameterisation
- `underconstrained`
  - too many things could be meant; more geometry or variable work is needed
- `degenerate`
  - the model collapses into vagueness or cannot distinguish the target behaviour from unlike behaviours
- `remodel needed`
  - the current family or combination may still matter, but only after structural revision

## Experiment 001

- `Experiment ID`: `EXP-001`
- `Date`: `2026-04-23`
- `Target behaviour`: glass transmission
- `Material or system class`: glass
- `Model family or family-combination`: `G + D + F`
- `Initial parameter intuition`:
  - moderate to high pathway permissibility
  - moderate dynamic coherence
  - meaningful defect sensitivity
- `Expected outcome`:
  - transmissive propagation through a disordered but still admissible whole
  - degraded clarity when local pathway continuity is disrupted by defects or impurities
- `Observed modelling judgment`:
  - promising
  - Family `G` appears necessary as the core propagation family
  - Family `D` may help explain why coherence remains possible without perfect lattice order
  - Family `F` looks essential for defect and impurity sensitivity
- `Status`: `promising`
- `Next move`:
  - define what counts as an admissible propagation pathway in geometric rather than verbal terms

## Experiment 003

- `Experiment ID`: `EXP-003`
- `Date`: `2026-04-23`
- `Target behaviour`: crystal vs glass propagation contrast
- `Material or system class`: crystal and glass
- `Model family or family-combination`: crystal `C + G + F`; glass `G + D + F`
- `Initial parameter intuition`:
  - crystal: stronger nested whole-order and more regular pathway structure
  - glass: weaker long-range order but still enough distributed admissibility for propagation
- `Expected outcome`:
  - both permit transmission, but by different structural grammars
- `Observed modelling judgment`:
  - coherent at a high level
  - the difference currently looks like structured pathway regularity versus distributed pathway permissibility
  - still underconstrained geometrically
- `Status`: `promising`
- `Next move`:
  - define a first geometry sketch for “regular pathway order” versus “distributed permissive order”
