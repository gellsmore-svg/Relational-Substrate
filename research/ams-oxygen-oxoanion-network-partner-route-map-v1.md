# AMS Oxygen Oxoanion Network Partner Route Map v1

## Purpose

This note maps oxygen's `O4` role:

```text
O4: phosphate, sulfate, nitrate, and oxoanion network partner
```

The oxygen family entry proposed:

```text
Oxygen carries polarity and oxidative power.
```

This file tests the third mechanism-facing claim: oxygen forms the exposed perimeter, distributed charge field, hydration interface, and geometric linkage network of major oxoanion systems such as phosphate, sulfate, nitrate, bicarbonate, and silicate.

The central claim is:

> In oxoanions, oxygen is not a peripheral add-on around a central atom. Oxygen is the outward-facing field that distributes charge, receives hydration, binds cations, governs transfer, and determines whether the larger unit behaves as storage group, structural network, signalling species, buffer system, or mineral framework.

## Source Anchors

The source base supports these facts:

- Phosphate groups in water are strongly coupled to hydration shells, ions, and electric fields, and phosphate oxygens interact directly with water and cations such as Mg2+.
- ATP commonly coordinates Mg2+ through its negatively charged phosphate oxygens in discrete bound states.
- Calcium phosphate mineral formation depends on phosphate geometry and oxygen-mediated aqueous interactions.
- Silicate glass and mineral structures are built from SiO4 tetrahedra linked through bridging oxygens, while non-bridging oxygens and modifiers alter network connectivity.
- Sulfated extracellular matrices use sulfate-rich oxygenated groups to create charged recognition environments.
- Bicarbonate and carbonate species participate in aqueous buffering and can enter radical/redox boundary chemistry under strong oxidant pressure.
- Nitrate and related oxyanions are charge-distributed oxygen-rich species whose biological behaviour depends on reduction, transport, and electrostatic compatibility rather than hydrophobic partitioning.

## Cross-Family Comparisons

### Oxygen and Phosphorus

This is one of the tightest pairings in the whole programme.

```text
phosphorus gives transfer-centred identity
oxygen gives exposed distributed charge and hydration interface
```

Without oxygen, phosphate would not be phosphate in any biologically useful sense.

### Oxygen and Sulfur

Sulfate differs from thiol/disulfide sulfur chemistry because oxygenation converts sulfur's behavioural emphasis.

```text
reduced sulfur -> controllable reactivity
sulfate oxygen perimeter -> fixed charged surface code
```

This is not a contradiction. It is a topology-dependent shift in what sulfur identity looks like once oxygen dominates the external field.

### Oxygen and Silicon

Silicon's network role is inseparable from oxygen linkage.

```text
silicon core without oxygen -> no silicate network grammar
oxygen bridging without silicon -> no tetrahedral silicate identity
```

This confirms that the earlier glass route maps were always partially oxygen stories, even when silicon was the family anchor.

### Oxygen and Carbon

Bicarbonate and carbonate show that carbon can enter strongly aqueous inorganic roles through oxygen perimeter control.

```text
O3 carbon file -> oxygen differentiates organic architecture
O4 oxoanion file -> oxygen externalises carbon into buffer/mineral chemistry
```

### Oxygen and Nitrogen

Nitrate shows a different nitrogen mode from amines, amides, and nucleobases.

```text
organic nitrogen -> recognition and availability
oxygenated nitrate nitrogen -> mobile ecological reservoir
```

The difference is not just oxidation state; it is a change in exposed field topology.
