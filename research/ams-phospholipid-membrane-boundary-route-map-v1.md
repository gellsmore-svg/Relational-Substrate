## Source-Supported Working Facts

The source literature supports these working facts:

```text
Phospholipids are fundamental building blocks of cell membranes.
Phospholipids are amphipathic molecules with hydrophilic phosphate-containing heads and hydrophobic fatty-acid tails.
In aqueous solution, phospholipids spontaneously form bilayers.
Hydrophobic tails face inward, away from water.
Hydrophilic heads face outward toward aqueous environments on both sides.
Phospholipid bilayers form stable barriers between aqueous compartments.
Membranes are fluid and elastic structures.
Membrane proteins are inserted into or associated with the lipid bilayer and carry out transport, signalling, and other specialised functions.
The fluid mosaic model describes membranes as protein-containing lipid bilayers with lateral mobility.
Hydrophobic molecules can pass through membranes more readily than hydrophilic molecules, while many hydrophilic or charged molecules require proteins or channels.
```
