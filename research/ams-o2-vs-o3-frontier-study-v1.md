# AMS O2 vs O3 Frontier Study v1

## Purpose

This document isolates the sharpest unresolved obstruction-character distinction in the current programme:

- `O2` late-gate obstruction
- `O3` pocketing obstruction

From:

- `ams-obstruction-character-benchmark-table-v1.md`

the main result was that these two classes are now the key obstruction-side frontier pair, especially in:

- `glass -> air`
- `glass -> crystal`

The reason this matters is that both obstruction classes can generate events that:

- look active
- look close to completion
- and can easily be confused with one another

But they are not the same thing.

This study aims to clarify:

- what each class actually does geometrically
- how each shapes route reality
- why one tends toward `R3` and the other toward `R5`
- how to tell them apart under the strongest benchmark conditions

## Core Claim

`O2` and `O3` are not two degrees of the same obstruction.

They differ in kind.

Current best distinction:

- `O2` preserves route logic for a while and then blocks it late
- `O3` reshapes the active zone into local pockets that preserve activity but weaken true onward route continuity

So:

- `O2` tends to produce failed real routes
- `O3` tends to produce pseudo-routes

That is the most important distinction in the document.

## Working Definitions

## `O2` Late-Gate Obstruction

An event progresses along a route that remains genuinely plausible through much of its path, but the route fails near completion because a later obstruction becomes decisive.

This means:

- route reality is real for a substantial part of the event
- failure is late rather than immediate
- the event is misleading because near-completion appears genuine

## `O3` Pocketing Obstruction

Obstruction shapes the active region into bounded local pockets that preserve activity and local coherence while weakening or preventing genuine onward completion.

This means:

- the event may look transitionally active
- but the active zone is biased toward local reseating rather than true completion
- pseudo-progression becomes likely

## Why the Distinction Matters

If the programme blurs `O2` and `O3`, it loses one of the best current explanations for why:

- some failures come from routes that were genuinely real but blocked late
- other failures come from locally active but never truly completion-directed behaviour

That distinction matters directly for:

- `R3`
- `R5`
- `L_escape`
- terminal-layer modelling
- compatibility-zone modelling

Because:

- `O2` is the obstruction-side engine of failed real routes
- `O3` is the obstruction-side engine of pseudo-routes

## Best Current Signatures

## `O2` Best Current Signature

- `J_trans`: medium or low/medium
- `Q_coh`: medium or medium/high early, then strained
- `D`: medium/high
- `L_escape`: real but weakening
- route shape: elongated or completion-directed enough to be genuinely promising

### Interpretation

The route is real enough to be worth attempting. The obstruction does not prevent route formation. It prevents route completion.

## `O3` Best Current Signature

- `J_bound`: medium/high
- `J_trans`: low/medium
- `Q_coh`: medium
- `D`: medium
- `L_escape`: overread because local activity is strong
- route shape: pocketed, bounded, locally active, weakly completion-directed

### Interpretation

The active zone preserves motion and coherence locally, but not true onward route viability.

## Main Structural Difference

Current best formulation:

- `O2` is route-preserving, then route-blocking
- `O3` is pocket-forming, then route-imitating

That is the cleanest obstruction-side distinction in the document.

## Geometric Contrast

## `O2` Geometry

### Spatial Logic

The active path remains elongated and progression-bearing for long enough that:

- completion looks real
- `L_escape` is genuinely present
- failure occurs after substantive progression

### Obstruction Behaviour

The obstruction acts late in the route:

- final narrowing fails
- terminal continuity breaks near the end
- receiving admissibility never finalises

### Main Associated Loop/Route Logic

- strongest association with `R3`
- often overlaps with `L3`

## `O3` Geometry

### Spatial Logic

The active region becomes pocketed or locally bounded:

- motion remains real
- local coherence remains real
- apparent advancement is mostly produced by repeated reseating inside the pocket

### Obstruction Behaviour

The obstruction does not mainly kill a route late. It reshapes the event into a local retention or pseudo-transition zone from relatively early on.

### Main Associated Loop/Route Logic

- strongest association with `R5`
- often overlaps with `L2`
- can contribute to shallow or bounded terminal-layer stalls

## Temporal Contrast

This distinction is also temporal.

## `O2`

Temporal feel:

- “the path was really going somewhere, then got stopped”

## `O3`

Temporal feel:

- “the event kept acting as if it were progressing, but mostly circulated locally”

This temporal contrast is one of the clearest ways to test the difference.

## Relation to `L_escape`

## `O2`

`L_escape` is real for longer.

That is one of the best reasons `O2` produces:

- `R3`

The event truly has completion-side viability for part of its life.

## `O3`

`L_escape` is frequently overestimated.

The event looks active and coherent, but pocketing makes:

- local activity

look like:

- route viability

when in fact true completion-side viability is weak.

### Main Consequence

This is the obstruction-side reason why:

- `R3`
- and `R5`

must remain separate.

## Benchmarks

## 1. Glass -> Air

### `O2` in Glass Release

Strong when:

- outward release genuinely begins
- the terminal path remains plausible
- the last part of the release process fails or closes

This produces:

- blocked release routes
- strong `R3`

### `O3` in Glass Release

Strong when:

- the terminal layer becomes locally pocketed
- release-like activity remains strong
- but onward release is mostly pseudo-progressive

This produces:

- trapped release
- pseudo-release loops
- strong `R5`

### Main Judgment

This is the best release-side test bed for the distinction.

## 2. Glass -> Crystal

### `O2` in Compatibility Stall

Strong when:

- compatibility narrowing begins genuinely
- a plausible handoff path exists
- the path fails late near admissibility completion

This produces:

- blocked compatibility routes
- strong `R3`

### `O3` in Compatibility Stall

Strong when:

- the compatibility zone becomes locally active
- narrowing-like behaviour appears
- but the transition is mostly pocketed pseudo-progression

This produces:

- pseudo-compatibility loops
- strong `R5`

### Main Judgment

This is the best cross-whole test bed for the distinction.

## 3. Crystal -> Air

### `O2` in Ordered Release

Plausible and important:

- release begins in a genuinely ordered way
- the route fails late

### `O3` in Ordered Release

Weaker and less central:

- pocketing is possible
- but crystal-like cases more often preserve route structure or redirect into ordered return

### Main Judgment

Useful contrast case, but not the strongest place to stabilise `O3`.

## Strongest Discriminators Between `O2` and `O3`

## 1. Does the Event Ever Preserve a Truly Completion-Directed Path for Long Enough to Matter?

If yes:

- likely `O2`

If not, and local activity dominates from relatively early:

- likely `O3`

## 2. Is the Obstruction Acting Late Along a Path or Early by Reshaping the Active Zone?

Late route-blocking:

- `O2`

Early pocket-forming:

- `O3`

## 3. Is `L_escape` Real or Mainly Inferred from Activity?

Real but weakening:

- `O2`

Apparent because of pocketed local activity:

- `O3`

## 4. Is Local Looping Secondary or Primary?

Secondary to failed progression:

- `O2`

Primary and self-maintaining:

- `O3`

## Relationship to Other Obstruction Classes

## `O2` vs `O1`

`O1` preserves one route successfully.

`O2` preserves route logic but blocks completion.

So `O2` sits downstream of selectivity and upstream of failure.

## `O3` vs `O5`

`O3` creates bounded local pseudo-progression.

`O5` weakens route dominance more diffusely.

So:

- `O3` is more local and loop-supporting
- `O5` is more spread and release-broadening

## `O3` vs `O6`

`O3` still preserves one dominant local activity pocket.

`O6` is what happens when even that pocket logic fragments.

So `O3` is a structured local failure, while `O6` is a breakdown regime.

## Strongest Current Judgments

## 1. `O2` Is the Obstruction Signature of `R3`

This is now the strongest obstruction-side conclusion.

Use `O2` when:

- the route is genuinely real
- the failure is late
- completion almost looks reachable

## 2. `O3` Is the Obstruction Signature of `R5`

Use `O3` when:

- local pocketing dominates
- activity is intense and bounded
- but the apparent route never becomes robustly real

## 3. Glass Cases Remain the Best Obstruction Frontier Test Bed

Again this is reinforced.

Glass:

- makes `O2` plausible
- makes `O3` plausible
- preserves enough activity for both to be visible without collapsing into simpler ordered return

- `O2`
- `O3`
- `R3`
- `R5`

under the same benchmark conditions.

That would finish the match between obstruction-side and route-side frontiers.
