# AMS Pilot T1B Corpus v1

## Purpose

This document is the first working corpus for the AMS substrate coherence programme.

It applies the framework, schema, pilot worksheet, and non-persistent topology atlas to a small but high-pressure set of cases.

This is not an exhaustive chemistry catalogue. It is a disciplined pilot.

## Included Pilot Elements In v1

This first corpus pass covers:

- `H`
- `C`
- `O`
- `Fe`

These four were chosen because together they stress:

- simple and polar bonding
- stable and unstable oxygen regimes
- chain and ring closure logic
- metallic and oxide transitions
- direct bridges toward T1C material wholes

## Cross-Case Patterns Emerging Already

### 1. Stable T1B cases are not all stable in the same way

The corpus already distinguishes:

- simple local paired closure (`H2`, `O2`)
- symmetric grouped closure (`CH4`)
- distributed ring closure (`C6H6`)
- metastable grouped closure (`O3`)
- network-supporting grouped closure (`SiO2`)
- mixed distributed oxide closure with strong T1C escalation (`Fe3O4`)

AMS should not flatten all stability into one kind of persistence.

### 2. The T1B to T1C boundary is real

Some cases remain clearly T1B.

Others quickly push toward T1C:

- `H2O`
- `SiO2`
- metallic iron
- `Fe3O4`

These are exactly the cases where higher-order identity must be tested rather than assumed.

### 3. Metastability matters early

`O3` already shows that the corpus cannot be organised around a simple stable / unstable binary.

### 4. Context-supported persistence is distinct from isolated persistence

`H3O+` and `FeCl3` both press this issue in different ways.

They suggest that some grouped micro-organisations are not best understood as simple isolated closures, but as context-sensitive viable states.

## Immediate Open Problems

The corpus has already exposed several research problems:

1. How exactly should AMS distinguish local grouped closure from early distributed whole formation?
2. What is the cleanest AMS treatment of delocalised or strongly distributed conventional bonding descriptions?
3. How should proton-transfer systems be mapped without collapsing into vague flow-language?
4. At what point does magnetic-order relevance become a T1C question rather than remaining T1B chemistry?
5. Which non-persistent topology classes best explain defect nucleation in network and oxide systems?
