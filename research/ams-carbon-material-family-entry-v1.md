## Source-Supported Working Facts

The source literature supports these working facts:

```text
Carbon has four valence electrons and commonly forms four covalent bonds.
Carbon can bond to itself, forming chains, branches, rings, and extended networks.
Carbon skeletons are central to organic molecules.
Biological macromolecules include proteins, nucleic acids, carbohydrates, and lipids, all built around carbon-containing structures.
Carbon can form single, double, and triple bonds, and can support different molecular geometries.
Isomerism allows the same atoms to be arranged into different molecules with different properties.
Carbon fixation converts inorganic carbon into organic matter.
The Calvin-Benson-Bassham cycle is globally dominant, but other bacterial and archaeal carbon-fixation pathways exist.
Carbon allotropes include diamond, graphite, graphene, nanotubes, fullerenes, and mixed sp2/sp3 structures.
Diamond and graphite show that the same element can produce radically different material properties through different bonding topology.
```

## T1B-to-T1C Implication

Carbon may become the central AMS continuity element.

It spans:

```text
T1B covalent bonding
-> T1B molecular skeletons
-> T1B functional-group chemistry
-> T1C metabolism
-> T1C macromolecules
-> T1C membranes
-> T1C biomass
-> T1D symbol-bearing molecules through nucleic acids
```

Key continuity:

```text
bonding geometry -> molecular identity -> polymer identity -> cell architecture -> inherited symbolic sequence
```

This makes carbon different from iron and sulfur.

Iron gives redox depth.

Sulfur gives controllable reactivity.

Carbon gives the architectural grammar in which much of life is built.

## Testable Expectations

If the AMS carbon model is coherent, the following should hold:

1. Carbon skeleton form should often matter as much as elemental composition.
2. Isomers should produce different route identities despite identical atom counts.
3. Biological polymers should depend on carbon-containing backbone continuity.
4. Lipid membranes should rely on carbon-chain hydrophobic architecture.
5. Carbon fixation should be a major T1B-to-T1C entry route.
6. Organic metabolism should repeatedly modify carbon skeleton oxidation state, branch structure, and functionalisation.
7. Aromatic and conjugated carbon systems should support stable electronic fields.
8. Carbon allotropes should show that topology alone can transform material identity.
