# AMS Obstruction Character Benchmark Table v1

## Purpose

This document applies the obstruction-character taxonomy from:

- `ams-obstruction-character-study-v1.md`

to the strongest benchmark cases in the programme.

The aims are:

- test whether obstruction character discriminates the benchmark cases cleanly
- identify which obstruction classes now function as anchors
- identify which obstruction classes remain the most valuable unresolved frontiers

## Obstruction Classes in Scope

- `O1` funnelling obstruction
- `O2` late-gate obstruction
- `O3` pocketing obstruction
- `O4` biasing obstruction
- `O5` diffusing obstruction
- `O6` fragmenting obstruction

## Benchmark Cases in Scope

- `air -> glass`
- `glass -> air`
- `air -> polished metal`
- `air -> oxidised metal`
- `glass -> crystal`
- `crystal -> air`

## Benchmark Table

| Benchmark case | Dominant obstruction classes | Secondary classes | Main current interpretation |
|---|---|---|---|
| air -> glass | `O1` weak/selective | `O5` in degraded entry | permissive entry with occasional selective narrowing or surface diffusion |
| glass -> air | `O2`, `O3`, `O5` | `O6` at stronger degradation | strongest mixed release frontier: blocked route, pseudo-route, and diffuse release all coexist |
| air -> polished metal | `O4` | weak `O2` in non-ideal surfaces | strongest biasing-obstruction anchor and clearest return-side case |
| air -> oxidised metal | `O2`, `O6` | weak `O4` where surface order partly survives | strongest degradation benchmark: apparent routes degrade into blocked and fragmenting behaviour |
| glass -> crystal | `O1`, `O2`, `O3` | weak `O5` in poorer compatibility zones | strongest compatibility frontier: selective routes, blocked routes, and pseudo-routes compete together |
| crystal -> air | `O1`, `O2`, `O4` | weak `O5` in rougher exits | strongest ordered-release comparison case: selectivity, late blocking, and return bias all matter |

## Case-by-Case Analysis

## 1. Air -> Glass

### Dominant Obstruction Character

- weak/selective `O1`

### Interpretation

This remains a broad handoff anchor rather than a high-obstruction case.

The role of obstruction is not mainly to block, but to:

- limit route spread enough for one viable continuation to dominate

That is why `O1` appears here in a lighter form rather than in an aggressive failure form.

### Secondary Character

- `O5` in degraded entry conditions

Where the surface weakens, broadening and weaker distributed continuation become more plausible.

### Judgment

This is a useful anchor for the mild, non-destructive side of obstruction logic.

## 2. Glass -> Air

### Dominant Obstruction Characters

- `O2`
- `O3`
- `O5`

### Interpretation

This is the richest current obstruction benchmark in the programme.

Why:

- `O2` explains blocked release routes
- `O3` explains pseudo-release loops and local retention
- `O5` explains diffuse release without full trapping or fragmentation

This is one of the strongest signs that the obstruction taxonomy is not decorative. It separates three different release-side failure logics inside one benchmark family.

### Secondary Character

- `O6` when degradation becomes strong enough to break route coherence entirely

### Judgment

This is the main release-side frontier for obstruction modelling.

## 3. Air -> Polished Metal

### Dominant Obstruction Character

- `O4`

### Interpretation

This remains one of the cleanest cases in the whole programme.

The obstruction is not best described as:

- “blocking outwardly”

in a generic sense. It is best described as:

- biasing the route field toward organised return

That is precisely what `O4` captures.

### Secondary Character

- weak `O2` only in less ideal surfaces where a route may seem present but fail under actual progression

### Judgment

This is the strongest anchor for `O4`.

## 4. Air -> Oxidised Metal

### Dominant Obstruction Characters

- `O2`
- `O6`

### Interpretation

This is the strongest degradation benchmark.

It shows how:

- some route logic can remain present long enough to appear promising (`O2`)
- while deeper coherence breaks apart into fragmenting route fields (`O6`)

This is exactly the kind of mixed degradation that the programme previously struggled to state clearly.

### Secondary Character

- weak `O4` where partial surface order survives

### Judgment

This is the clearest current benchmark for the transition from blocked apparent route into fragmentation.

## 5. Glass -> Crystal

### Dominant Obstruction Characters

- `O1`
- `O2`
- `O3`

### Interpretation

This remains the richest compatibility benchmark.

It needs:

- `O1` because successful compatibility often depends on preserving one narrow viable route
- `O2` because many routes fail late under real progression
- `O3` because some local compatibility activity becomes pseudo-progressive looping rather than true admissible handoff

### Secondary Character

- weak `O5` in lower-quality compatibility zones where broad weakening replaces sharper local structures

### Judgment

This is the best cross-whole frontier case for obstruction modelling.

## 6. Crystal -> Air

### Dominant Obstruction Characters

- `O1`
- `O2`
- `O4`

### Interpretation

Crystal exit preserves route selectivity and directional organisation better than glass.

That is why:

- `O1` matters for viable narrow release routes
- `O2` matters for failed ordered release
- `O4` matters for ordered return

Pseudo-route pocketing is less central here.

### Secondary Character

- weak `O5` in rougher or less ideal exit conditions

### Judgment

This remains the strongest ordered-release comparison case.

## Which Obstruction Classes Are Now Anchors?

## Anchor Obstruction Classes

### `O4` Biasing Obstruction

Why:

- clearest and cleanest benchmark:
  - `air -> polished metal`
- strong explanatory stability
- strong directional meaning

### `O1` Funnelling Obstruction

Why:

- appears in:
  - `air -> glass`
  - `glass -> crystal`
  - `crystal -> air`
- helps explain selective viability without generic blockage

These two are now the strongest anchor obstruction classes.

## Which Obstruction Classes Are Frontier Classes?

## Main Frontier Classes

### `O2` Late-Gate Obstruction

Why:

- strongest generator of `R3`
- central to blocked apparent routes
- appears across release and compatibility work

### `O3` Pocketing Obstruction

Why:

- strongest generator of `R5`
- central to pseudo-routes and shallow/local retention
- strongest in glass release and compatibility activity

These are now the main unresolved obstruction frontiers.

## Supporting Frontier Class

### `O6` Fragmenting Obstruction

Why:

- it marks the breakdown edge
- strongest in oxidised or severely degraded cases

This matters, but it is slightly less central than `O2` and `O3` because it operates more often as a collapse regime than as the main unresolved structured frontier.

## Strongest Cross-Benchmark Findings

## 1. The Obstruction Taxonomy Survives the Main Cases

This is the main result.

The classes do not collapse when applied to the benchmark set. They sharpen it.

## 2. `O2` and `O3` Are the True Frontier Pair

This is the most important strategic outcome.

They now look to obstruction modelling the way:

- `R3`
- `R5`

looked to route modelling.

That is a very good sign.

## 3. Glass Cases Remain the Best Frontier Test Bed

Again this is reinforced.

Glass:

- supports blocked routes
- supports pseudo-routes
- supports diffuse release

without collapsing immediately into simpler organised return.

## 4. The Reflection Side Is Now Stable

`O4` gives the polished-metal case stable explanatory footing.

That means future work does not need to keep revisiting the basic reflection anchor unless new evidence pressures it.

## 5. Degradation Is Now Better Understood

The oxidised-metal case now has a clearer obstruction reading:

- some route logic remains temporarily (`O2`)
- deeper route coherence fragments (`O6`)

This is an important gain.

## Strategic Consequences

## 1. The Next Deep Pass Should Target `O2` vs `O3`

This is the clearest conclusion.

Why:

- they are the obstruction-side counterparts to the earlier `R3` / `R5` frontier
- they sit exactly where the model still needs the most sharpening

## 2. Glass -> Air and Glass -> Crystal Remain the Best Frontier Pair

Because together they carry:

- `O2`
- `O3`
- and secondary `O5`

in the strongest and most revealing combination.

## 3. No New Obstruction Classes Are Needed Yet

The current six-class set is sufficient for now.

The next gains should come from deeper discrimination, not more categories.
