# AMS Score Recalibration Log v4

## Purpose

This document records the fourth formal recalibration round for the AMS substrate coherence programme.

Rounds 1 to 3 were driven by:

- first T1C scaling
- extended T1C whole formation
- boundary, interface, defect, hysteresis, and retained-history pressure

Round 4 is driven by:

- teleology
- function-bearing wholeness
- role-bearing persistence

This is a stricter test than earlier rounds because some cases now gain support not only by persisting, but by showing that their organised role belongs to what they are.

## Round 4 Principle

A score should rise in this round only where the corpus now supports all or most of the following:

- stable higher-order whole identity
- meaningful structural role
- function that is more than observer shorthand
- stronger teleological significance tied to the identity of the whole

Scores should not rise simply because a case is interesting or useful. The teleological pressure must actually deepen the AMS interpretation.

## Round 4 Updates

### 1. Membranes

- `Old score`: `8/10`
- `New score`: `9/10`
- `Reason`:
  - the teleology corpus strengthened the claim that membrane function is not a decorative feature added to a stable whole
  - boundary, regulation, and selective transport now look constitutive rather than merely associated
  - the case now stands out as one of the clearest examples of a function-bearing higher-order whole
- `Stability`: strong

### 2. Biological Macromolecular Assemblies

- `Old score`: `7/10`
- `New score`: `8/10`
- `Reason`:
  - the teleology corpus strengthened the claim that these assemblies do not merely persist, but persist in role-sensitive and function-sensitive ways
  - loss of organised role now appears more clearly as loss of what the whole is, not merely loss of external usefulness
- `Stability`: provisional but strong

### 3. Water Networks In Living Contexts

- `Old score`: `8/10` for water networks as a structural T1C case
- `New score`: retained at `8/10`
- `Reason`:
  - the teleology corpus increased their contextual significance in living systems
  - but the functional significance remains strongly derivative through larger wholes rather than intrinsic enough to justify a score rise at this stage
- `Stability`: stable

### 4. Catalytic Surfaces

- `Old score`: `6/10`
- `New score`: `7/10`
- `Reason`:
  - the teleology corpus strengthened the claim that catalytic function is structurally grounded and boundary-concentrated rather than merely an observer convenience
  - however, the case still remains weaker than living teleological wholes because the role is not yet as tightly inseparable from the identity of the whole
- `Stability`: provisional

### 5. Organismal Boundary And Regulation

- `Old score`: `8/10`
- `New score`: `9/10`
- `Reason`:
  - this is now one of the clearest cases in the programme where function, persistence, boundary, and identity converge
  - the whole ceases to be what it is if the organised regulatory role fails
- `Stability`: strong

## Round 4 Summary

Round 4 produces three main conclusions:

1. The programme now clearly distinguishes strong structural wholes from strong function-bearing wholes.
2. Some cases rise because their organised role is part of their identity, not merely because they are stable.
3. The ontology is becoming more differentiated without fragmenting, which is exactly what this research programme needed.
