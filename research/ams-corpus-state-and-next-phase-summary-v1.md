# AMS Corpus State and Next-Phase Summary v1

## Purpose

This note summarises the current state of the AMS research corpus after the recent hardening phase.

The goal is not to restate every file. It is to answer four questions clearly:

```text
1. What now exists?
2. What has materially improved?
3. What remains weakest?
4. What should the next phase focus on?
```

This is the programme-control summary for the current stage.

## Central Claim

The central claim is:

> The AMS corpus has moved from an ambitious but uneven conceptual programme into a much more structured research body with three real strengths: a locked comparative element-family core, a layered runtime ontology from machines to persons, and a first semi-formal substrate scaffold. The next phase should focus less on raw expansion and more on calibration, reintegration, and disciplined extension from the strengthened core.

## Current Corpus State

The corpus now has five major layers.

## 1. Element-Family Core

The strongest locked or near-locked family identities are:

```text
Carbon carries molecular architecture.
Nitrogen carries biological availability and recognition.
Phosphorus carries transferable biological order.
Sulfur carries controllable chemical reactivity.
Iron carries redox depth.
Oxygen carries polarity and oxidative power.
```

Current standing:

```text
Carbon: 9.5/10 locked
Nitrogen: 9.5/10 locked
Phosphorus: 9.5/10 locked
Sulfur: 9.0/10 locked
Iron: 9.25/10 provisional upward revision
Oxygen: 9.5/10 locked
```

This is now the clearest empirical-comparative centre of gravity in the project.

## 2. Runtime-Machine Layer

Two core runtime machines are now strongly mapped:

```text
Respiration / ATP synthesis
Ribosome / translation
```

These now exist not only as narrative descriptions, but as:

- integrated route maps;
- comparative synthesis;
- semi-quantitative calibration;
- failure/dependency propagation structures.

This is a major step up in rigour.

## 3. Boundary Layer

The ontology ladder is now substantially clearer:

```text
machine
-> life
-> person
```

With dedicated files for:

- living-state boundary;
- AI versus living systems;
- personhood versus living systems;
- life edge cases;
- personhood edge cases.

This is one of the strongest recent conceptual gains.

## 4. Arc Layer

The upper ontology now has dedicated files for:

- corruption as runtime fragmentation;
- redemption as restorative reordering;
- restoration versus replacement;
- resurrection continuity;
- full creation-corruption-redemption synthesis.

This layer was previously one of the weakest.
It is now materially stronger, though still more synthetic than the machine and family core.

## 5. Formal Scaffold Layer

The programme now has a first semi-formal substrate scaffold:

- vorton geometry and closure formalism;
- route variable framework;
- topology transition criteria;
- mapping from formal scaffold back into element-family profiles.

This is a major shift.

The problem is no longer:

```text
no formal scaffold exists
```

It is now:

```text
the formal scaffold exists but needs calibration
```

That is substantial progress.

## What Has Materially Improved

The recent work has improved the corpus in four especially important ways.

## 1. The Top of the Ontology Is Less Loose

Previously the higher arc:

- life;
- personhood;
- corruption;
- redemption;

was more vulnerable to the criticism that it outran the lower-level work.

That is now less true because:

- personhood has edge-case hardening;
- corruption has a comparison matrix;
- restoration is distinguished from replacement;
- resurrection has a continuity stress test;
- the full thesis has a refreshed `v2`.

## 2. The Middle of the Ontology Is Stronger

The machine layer is now not only illustrative but comparative and partially calibrated.

That matters because it shows the programme can:

- connect family identities to real systems;
- distinguish machine types structurally;
- describe failure propagation rather than only healthy function.

## 3. The Bottom of the Ontology Is More Constrained

The formal scaffold is still incomplete, but the ontology now has:

- defined objects;
- defined variables;
- defined transition classes;
- defined family mappings.

That prevents the substrate discussion from remaining entirely intuitive.

## 4. The Comparative Core Is Sharper

The main family-edge cases have now been materially hardened:

- iron non-heme catalysis;
- sulfur sulfation-pattern specificity;
- oxygen planetary source-sink logic;
- phosphorus pathological mislocation and calcification.

That means the strongest central comparative core is less exposed at the margins than before.

## What Remains Weakest

Even after the hardening phase, the weakest areas are still identifiable.

## 1. Calibration of the Formal Scaffold

The most important remaining weakness is now:

```text
formal calibration
```

not:

```text
formal absence
```

The scaffold exists, but still needs:

- tighter quantitative grounding;
- stronger case calibration;
- more explicit dependency equations or weighted models;
- better discriminants between rival route interpretations.

## 2. Remaining Family Context-Sensitivity

The family core is strong, but:

- sulfur remains more context-sensitive than the cleanest families;
- iron still has breadth and governance dependence;
- oxygen is strongest locally and more cautious at planetary scale.

This is manageable, but still real.

## 3. Summary-Layer Reintegration

The corpus now contains stronger local files than some of its earlier summary files reflected.

This has improved with:

- `ams-full-ontology-core-thesis-synthesis-v2.md`
- `ams-c-n-p-s-fe-o-cross-family-synthesis-v2.md`
- `ams-priority-roadmap-refresh-v2.md`

but further reintegration will still be needed as the next phase proceeds.

## 4. Outward Extension Risk

The programme is now better prepared for:

- social;
- ecclesial;
- civilisational;

applications, but these remain the highest-risk zone for premature extension.

They should still be delayed until calibration is stronger.

## Next-Phase Priorities

The next phase should focus on three things.

## 1. Calibration

Highest-value targets:

- `ams-core-runtime-machines-quantitative-comparison-v1.md` follow-on work;
- stronger dependency matrices and failure weighting;
- tighter formal case calibration.

This remains the single highest-value class of work.

## 2. Remaining Family Edge Closure

The major current candidate here is:

```text
iron final re-lock or broader family reconciliation
```

with sulfur and oxygen planetary-scale caution still worth watching.

## 3. Controlled Reintegration

Future summary updates should happen:

- after meaningful calibration gains;
- not just after more local file multiplication.

That keeps the summary layer honest.

## What Not to Do Next

The corpus is now large enough that bad next moves matter.

Avoid prioritising:

- broad new element families before calibration improves;
- social/civilisational extrapolation too early;
- additional top-level synthesis with no new constraint;
- purely stylistic restatement of already strong claims.
