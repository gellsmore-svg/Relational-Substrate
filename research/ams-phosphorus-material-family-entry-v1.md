# AMS Phosphorus Material Family Entry v1

## Purpose

This entry opens the phosphorus material-family workstream.

Phosphorus is a high-value AMS family because it connects:

- phosphate minerals
- hydroxyapatite and bone
- DNA/RNA backbone
- ATP and energy transfer
- phospholipids and membranes
- phosphorylation and signalling
- pH buffering and metabolic regulation
- T1B mineral order to T1C biological function

The central AMS question is:

```text
How can phosphate carry structure, energy, information, boundary formation, and regulation across living systems?
```

## Source-Supported Working Facts

The source literature supports these working facts:

```text
Phosphorus is most commonly present biologically as phosphate, PO4^3-.
Phosphate is essential to all known life.
Phosphorus is a structural component of DNA and RNA.
Phosphorus is a structural component of phospholipids in plasma and organelle membranes.
ATP and other phosphorylated nucleotides store and transfer cellular energy.
Phosphorylation is central to cellular regulation and enzymatic control.
Phosphate participates in pH buffering and metabolic reactions.
Calcium phosphate / hydroxyapatite mineralizes bone and teeth.
Most adult body phosphorus is stored in bone, while a smaller inorganic phosphate pool is crucial for cellular and extracellular function.
Matrix vesicles use phosphate to form hydroxyapatite crystals during bone mineralization.
Phosphate excess or misregulation can contribute to pathological calcification and cellular dysfunction.
```



Interpretation:

