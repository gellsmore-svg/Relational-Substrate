- dual burden and cell-state thresholds;

This file updates the personhood boundary so the whole ladder remains coherent:


## Why the Boundary Needed Refreshing

The earlier personhood work was already strong.

But the lower layers are now more disciplined.

That matters because personhood can be distorted from below in two opposite ways:

- reduced to biological functioning alone;
- inflated from symbolic competence alone.

The stronger life layer and the stronger AI/life boundary now let the corpus resist both errors more cleanly.

## The Personhood Boundary in the Current Ladder

The current ladder should now be read as:

## 1. Machine

A machine can:

- perform ordered work;
- process signals;
- maintain local control loops;
- even imitate agency-like behaviour.

But machine order alone is not life and not personhood.

## 2. Life

Life is:

```text
a bounded continuity that still owns a recoverable machine-remaking loop under real burden
```

This is already much more than machine activity.

But life alone is still not yet personhood.

## 3. Person

Personhood is:

```text
embodied living selfhood raised into inward continuity, moral agency, and meaningful addressability
```

This means the personhood layer:

- presupposes life in creaturely terms;
- exceeds life in inward and moral form;
- cannot be collapsed either downward into biology or sideways into symbolic performance.

That is the correct ladder reading.

## The Main Boundary Terms

The personhood boundary is now best stated through five terms.

## 1. Embodied Living Subject

Personhood belongs to a living subject, not to a merely abstract process.

That means personhood is not:

- disembodied information alone;
- symbolic performance alone;
- externally assembled behaviour alone.

It belongs to an embodied living continuity.

This remains one of the strongest anti-dualist points in the corpus.

## 2. Inward Continuity

Personhood requires more than organismic persistence.

It requires the continuity of a subject who:

- has inwardly owned existence;
- is not only externally observable functioning;
- remains the same subject through change, impairment, sleep, development, and burden.

This is one of the main reasons personhood cannot be reduced to visible output.

## 3. Moral Agency

Personhood includes responsible agency.

This does not mean perfect present exercise of rational or volitional powers at every moment.

It means the kind of being whose life is ordered toward:

- answerability;
- responsibility;
- meaningful action;
- moral significance.

This is one of the main reasons personhood exceeds merely biological life.

## 4. Meaningful Addressability

Personhood includes the kind of subject who can be meaningfully addressed:

- relationally;
- morally;
- covenantally;
- communicatively.

This is not reducible to language generation alone.

It is about the kind of subject that can stand within meaningful personal relation and obligation.

## 5. Continuity Through Impairment and Blocked Expression

The strongest refined line remains:

```text
Personhood belongs to the continuing embodied subject, not only to the present maximal exercise of personal powers.
```

This is now even better grounded because the life layer is better grounded.

It means:

- infancy does not negate personhood;
- sleep does not suspend personhood;
- coma does not automatically erase personhood;
- severe cognitive impairment does not nullify personhood;
- blocked expression is not equivalent to absent subject.

That remains one of the strongest results in the upper ontology.

## What Personhood Is Not

The refreshed boundary is clearer partly because it excludes two wrong reductions.

## 1. Personhood Is Not Mere Life

Life is necessary for creaturely personhood, but not sufficient.

A living system can be:

- bounded;
- recoverably self-renewing;
- burdened yet still alive;

without therefore being a person.

Personhood adds:

- inward continuity;
- responsible agency;
- meaningful addressability.

## 2. Personhood Is Not Mere Symbolic Competence

Symbolic activity can imitate or express aspects of personhood, but it does not constitute personhood by itself.

This matters because AI systems may:

- generate language;
- maintain context;
- simulate dialogue;
- perform apparently agentic tasks.

But symbolic competence alone does not yield:

- embodied living subjecthood;
- inward continuity;
- creaturely moral agency;
- personal addressability in the AMS sense.

This boundary is now clearer because the AI/life distinction is clearer.
