# AMS Iron Material Family Entry v1

## Purpose

This entry opens the iron material-family workstream.

Iron is the paired counterpart to sulfur in the previous sulfur synthesis:

```text
Iron supplies redox depth; sulfur supplies redox closure.
```

Iron is a high-value AMS family because it connects:

- heme;
- oxygen transport;
- mitochondrial respiration;
- cytochromes;
- iron-sulfur clusters;
- DNA metabolism;
- ferritin storage;
- transferrin transport;
- hepcidin/ferroportin systemic control;
- ferroptosis and Fenton chemistry;
- magnetite and greigite biomineralization;
- hydrothermal and mineral-redox chemistry.

The central AMS question is:

```text
How does iron carry deep redox capacity without destroying the systems that use it?
```

## Source-Supported Working Facts

The source literature supports these working facts:

```text
Iron is essential for most life.
Iron cycles between Fe2+ and Fe3+, giving biological redox utility.
That same redox cycling makes iron toxic when uncontrolled.
Most human iron is bound in heme.
Iron also occurs in Fe-S clusters and non-heme iron proteins.
Heme supports oxygen transport, gas sensing, oxidative metabolism, mitochondrial respiration, detoxification, and signalling.
Fe-S proteins support redox reactions, catalysis, respiration, DNA replication, transcription, and structural functions.
Iron homeostasis depends on tightly regulated absorption, transport, storage, utilisation, export, recycling, and degradation.
Transferrin transports ferric iron in circulation.
Ferritin stores iron safely.
Ferroportin exports iron.
Hepcidin regulates systemic iron availability through ferroportin control.
Excess labile iron promotes ROS generation, Fenton chemistry, lipid peroxidation, and ferroptosis.
Magnetotactic bacteria biomineralize iron into magnetite or greigite nanoparticles inside magnetosomes.
Magnetosome formation involves controlled iron concentration, pH, redox state, nucleation, crystal size, crystal shape, and chain organisation.
```

## Testable Expectations

If the AMS iron model is coherent, the following should hold:

1. Iron's most useful roles should occur when it is embedded in a governing structure: heme, Fe-S cluster, enzyme pocket, ferritin, transferrin, magnetosome.
2. Free or labile iron should correlate with increased oxidative side chemistry.
3. Heme and Fe-S systems should show distinct route grammars despite sharing iron redox depth.
4. Storage and transport systems should be as central to iron biology as catalytic systems.
5. Iron deficiency should disrupt oxygen transport, respiration, DNA metabolism, and mitochondrial function.
6. Iron overload should increase oxidative damage and ferroptotic vulnerability.
7. Magnetotactic bacteria should require tight control over iron concentration, redox state, pH, nucleation, crystal size, crystal morphology, and chain alignment.
8. Iron-sulfur systems should remain the strongest bridge between iron and sulfur family models.
