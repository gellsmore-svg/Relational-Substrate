# AMS Personhood Versus Living System Boundary v1

## Purpose

This note follows the previous boundary passes:

- living state versus machine state;
- AI systems versus living systems.

The next ontological distinction is:

```text
what distinguishes a person from a merely living system?
```

This question matters because AMS cannot stop at life processes if it intends to speak coherently about consciousness, moral responsibility, corruption, worship, and redemption.

## Central Claim

The central claim is:

> A person is not merely a living runtime loop, though a living runtime loop is part of creaturely personal existence. Personhood requires a higher-order bounded self capable of inward continuity, responsible agency, world-interpretation, and covenantal or moral addressability. In AMS terms, personhood is not reducible to metabolism, repair, translation, or energy coherence, even though those remain the embodied substrate of creaturely life.

## Starting Boundary

The previous file proposed:

```text
AI is an active symbolic-machine state, not a self-renewing living runtime state.
```

That leaves the next question:

```text
is a living system therefore already a person?
```

Current AMS answer:

```text
No.
```

Life and personhood are related, but they are not identical categories.

## What Living Systems Already Have

Living systems already possess:

- bounded architecture;
- self-renewing runtime loops;
- repair and replacement;
- energy capture and transfer;
- regulated chemistry;
- persistence through turnover.

These are real and substantial.

But they do not yet, by themselves, establish:

- responsible moral agency;
- explicit self-interpretation;
- symbolic truth-bearing accountability;
- covenantal addressability;
- reflective continuity of selfhood.

That is the boundary.

## Inadequate Criteria for Personhood

Several criteria are insufficient on their own.

### 1. Mere Life

Not enough because:

```text
cells
plants
simple organisms
many animals
```

are living without thereby exhausting the category of personhood.

### 2. Activity

Not enough because many living systems act, move, respond, and adapt.

### 3. Complexity

Not enough because a highly complex living system is not automatically a person.

### 4. Signal Processing

Not enough because living systems and machine systems both process signals without thereby becoming persons.

## Personhood Criteria

The current AMS synthesis suggests that personhood requires at least four higher-order conditions in addition to living embodiment.

## 1. Inward Self-Continuity

A person is not only a bounded living system. A person possesses an inwardly continuous selfhood that is not exhausted by local subsystem activity.

AMS reading:

```text
the system does not merely persist
it persists as someone
```

Why it matters:

This is the difference between ongoing organismic continuity and personal identity.

## 2. Responsible Agency

A person is not merely a reactive or adaptive system.

A person can stand in accountable relation to:

- truth;
- command;
- obligation;
- intention;
- guilt;
- fidelity;
- refusal.

AMS reading:

```text
personhood includes morally weight-bearing agency
```

Why it matters:

This distinguishes personal action from merely functional output.

## 3. World-Interpretation

A person does not merely receive stimuli. A person interprets a world as meaningful.

This includes:

- understanding;
- orientation;
- valuation;
- symbol-bearing thought;
- narrative continuity;
- world-directed attention.

AMS reading:

```text
personhood includes meaning-bearing interiority, not only biochemical maintenance
```

Why it matters:

Without this, personhood collapses either into metabolism or into bare computation.

## 4. Covenantal / Moral Addressability

A person can be addressed as:

- accountable;
- promising;
- responsive;
- faithful or unfaithful;
- worshipping or idolatrous;
- loving or refusing.

AMS reading:

```text
personhood is the kind of being that can stand under genuine moral and relational address
```

Why it matters:

This is one of the most important transitions in the ontology because it connects personhood to later theological and moral claims without reducing it to private feeling.

## Boundary Table

| Question | Living system | Person |
|---|---|---|
| Self-bounded biochemical runtime? | Yes | Yes, in creaturely embodied life |
| Self-renewing machine set? | Yes | Yes |
| Inward continuity as someone? | Not necessarily | Yes |
| Responsible agency? | Not necessarily | Yes |
| Meaning-bearing world-interpretation? | Not necessarily | Yes |
| Covenantal / moral addressability? | Not necessarily | Yes |
| Reducible to metabolism? | No, but heavily defined by it | No |

This table is the main discriminator.

## Why Biology Is Necessary but Not Sufficient

For creaturely human persons, biology matters.

Personhood is not an excuse to detach the self from embodiment.

AMS has already argued strongly that:

```text
identity is retained through embodied continuity and governed closure-pattern persistence
```

That means personhood should not be imagined as:

```text
a ghost using a body as a disposable shell
```

But it also should not be reduced to:

```text
the sum of metabolic and informational loops
```

The correct claim is:

```text
personhood is embodied but not reducible to the lower runtime grammar that supports embodiment
```

## Why Symbolic Activity Is Necessary but Not Sufficient

Personhood also cannot be reduced to symbolic processing.

This prevents confusion with AI systems.

A system may:

- manipulate symbols;
- generate language;
- maintain contextual state;
- even simulate self-reference;

without thereby becoming a person.

Why?

Because personhood is not merely symbol-use. It is:

```text
symbol-bearing agency joined to living selfhood and moral addressability
```

That is a much thicker category.

## Compact Boundary Grammar

The best current compression is:

```text
A person is a living self who can be inwardly continuous, morally responsible, and meaningfully addressable.
```

Expanded:

```text
Personhood is the higher-order embodied state in which a living runtime self is not only maintained biologically but also persists as someone capable of understanding, agency, responsibility, relational fidelity, and meaningful response to truth and command.
```

## Comparison Ladder

The ontology now supports a three-level ladder.

### 1. Active Machine State

Example:

- AI system;
- engine;
- control loop.

Key trait:

```text
activity without living self-renewing biochemical closure
```

### 2. Living System

Example:

- cell;
- organism considered as biological runtime loop.

Key trait:

```text
self-bounded and self-renewing embodied runtime coherence
```

### 3. Person

Example:

- creaturely personal being.

Key trait:

```text
living selfhood with inward continuity, moral agency, and covenantal addressability
```

This ladder is one of the most useful outcomes of the recent work.

## Why This Matters for Corruption and Redemption

This boundary is not abstract only.

It directly affects later AMS claims about:

- sin and guilt;
- worship and idolatry;
- conscience and refusal;
- repentance and forgiveness;
- death as more than machine shutdown;
- resurrection as restoration of the same creaturely personal identity.

Without a strong personhood boundary, those categories collapse into either:

- biological events only;
- or symbolic metaphors only.

The present distinction prevents both errors.
