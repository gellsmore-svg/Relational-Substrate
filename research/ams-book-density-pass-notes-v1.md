# AMS Book Density Pass Notes v1

## Purpose

This file records the density pass across the current drafted chapter sequence.

The purpose of the density pass is to assess:

- where the prose is too compressed to carry the reader well;
- where it is too dense for narrative flow;
- where technical weight is still too visible;
- where further compression would help without weakening the argument.

The aim is not to flatten the manuscript.

The aim is:

```text
to keep the strongest conceptual force while making the reading load proportionate to each chapter's job
```

## Central Claim

The central claim is:

> The current draft is conceptually strong and generally readable, but its density is still uneven. The strongest chapters already balance force and clarity well, while the middle ontological bridge chapters and the final arc chapters would benefit from selective compression of explanatory scaffolding and slightly more breathing room at key conceptual pivots. The density problem is therefore local and editorial, not structural.

More briefly:

```text
The draft is not too heavy overall, but some chapters carry their weight too continuously.
```

## Chapter 3: Machine Order

### Density judgment

Chapter 3 is one of the heaviest middle chapters.

It works conceptually, but it carries a lot continuously.

### Main density risk

The biggest risk is that:

- machine definition
- respiration
- translation
- threshold behaviour

all arrive in one steady block of high-pressure explanation.

## Chapter 4: Life as Recoverable Ecology

### Density judgment

Chapter 4 is conceptually excellent, but currently one of the densest chapters in the draft.

That is understandable because it carries a major transition and many distinctions.

### Main density risk

The chapter contains, in close sequence:

- machine versus life
- recoverability
- reserve
- support
- acute and cumulative burden
- death approach
- edge-case implications

This can feel cumulative in the strong sense, but also tiring if not relieved slightly.

### Recommended density adjustment

- preserve the life definition and the stress/death distinction
- trim one layer of repeated “visible activity is not enough” explanation
- consider a slightly shorter paragraph around reserve/support if assembled prose allows

### Density score

```text
8.4/10
```
