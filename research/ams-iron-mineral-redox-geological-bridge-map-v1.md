## Source Anchors

The source base supports these facts:

- Iron is central to Earth redox history and biogeochemistry.
- Banded iron formations record massive iron mineral deposition in early oceans, often linked to anoxic, iron-rich marine conditions and changing oxidation regimes.
- BIFs contain iron minerals including magnetite, hematite, siderite, and other iron-bearing phases.
- Multiple mechanisms have been proposed for ancient Fe(II) oxidation, including oxygen, microbial oxidation, photo-oxidation, peroxide, and abiotic anoxic pathways.
- Ferruginous marine conditions persisted through major portions of Earth history.
- Hydrothermal systems provide redox, pH, temperature, mineral, and chemical gradients.
- Metal sulfides, including FeS, can participate in geoelectrochemical reactions relevant to protometabolic chemistry.
- Iron sulfides have been proposed as important prebiotic catalysts and electron conductors.
- Greigite (`Fe3S4`) has a cubane-like structural relationship to biological `[4Fe4S]` clusters.
- Iron oxide and iron sulfide nanomaterials can show oxidoreductase-like activity and are found in diverse environments and biological contexts.

## Mechanism 1: Fe2+/Fe3+ Geological Redox Cycling

Iron cycles between ferrous and ferric states in minerals, waters, sediments, and biological systems.

AMS route:

```text
reduced Fe2+
-> oxidising pressure / microbial route / photochemical route / abiotic route
-> Fe3+ mineral formation
-> sedimentary or catalytic redox record
```

Classification:

```text
R2/R4/O4/D4/G_B/G_A
```

Why:

At geological scale, the route is real but distributed. It is not a single enzyme route. It is a field of oxidation, precipitation, transport, and burial.

AMS variables:

```text
B_in    = Fe2+ supply and redox disequilibrium
Q_coh   = environmental coherence: pH, Eh, ligand field, microbial/abiotic context
J_bound = iron retained in mineral phases
J_trans = electron transfer / oxidation-reduction pathway
B_out   = iron oxide/sulfide/carbonate deposition or biological uptake
I_comp  = dilution, competing ligands, sulfidation, silica, carbonate, organic matter
D       = D4
```

## Mechanism 2: Banded Iron Formation Route

Banded iron formations are Earth-scale redox memory structures. They record episodes of iron-rich ocean chemistry, oxidation, precipitation, and sedimentation.

AMS route:

```text
Fe-rich ocean/hydrothermal input
-> oxidation/precipitation regime
-> iron mineral deposition
-> banded sedimentary archive
```

Classification:

```text
R2/R4/O4/D4/G_B
```

Why:

BIFs are not a single route but a preserved environmental outcome. They show iron's capacity to carry redox history into mineral form.

AMS interpretation:

```text
BIFs are geological redox-memory fields.
```

Caution:

The mechanism of Fe(II) oxidation in ancient BIF formation is debated. AMS should treat BIFs as strong evidence for iron redox/deposition significance, not as proof of one exclusive oxidation mechanism.

## Boundary Conditions

The `I8` model must remain disciplined.

Strong claims:

- iron minerals are central to Earth redox chemistry;
- BIFs preserve major iron redox/deposition history;
- Fe-S minerals and biological Fe-S clusters form a strong continuity motif;
- hydrothermal mineral systems provide plausible redox-gradient route fields;
- biological iron systems internalise and govern iron redox depth.

Restricted claims:

- one exclusive mechanism for BIF formation;
- one settled origin-of-life pathway;
- automatic conversion from mineral catalysis to metabolism;
- exact reconstruction of Hadean conditions from modern analogues.

AMS score discipline depends on this distinction.
