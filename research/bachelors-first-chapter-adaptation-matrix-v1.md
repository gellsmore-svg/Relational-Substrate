## Purpose

This matrix maps the current 15-chapter canonical master to a first derived `Bachelor’s edition`.

The Bachelor’s edition should:
- preserve the full argument
- reduce hidden dependency chains
- maintain stable terminology
- lower local density spikes
- add visible chapter stakes and transition discipline
- keep examples only where they improve comprehension or demonstrate explanatory gain

## Adaptation Codes

- `KEEP` = preserve nearly as-is
- `TIGHTEN` = reduce repetition, sentence load, or local density
- `BRIDGE` = add short orientation or dependency-marking prose
- `SPLIT` = subdivide long sections or paragraphs without changing chapter identity
- `SHIFT APPENDIX` = keep main claim but move denser stress-test detail to appendix emphasis
- `STATUS TAG` = mark more clearly what is baseline ontology versus mechanism proposal
