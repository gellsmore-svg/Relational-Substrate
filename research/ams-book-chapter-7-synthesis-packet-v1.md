# AMS Book Chapter 7 Synthesis Packet v1

## Purpose

This file prepares the actual synthesis packet for Chapter 7 of the book.

Chapter 6 has now established:

```text
corruption as fragmentation within continuity
```

The task of Chapter 7 is therefore the necessary next step:

```text
show that redemption is the restorative healing of that same damaged continuity
```

This file turns that chapter slot into a usable drafting packet.

## Central Claim

The central claim is:

> Chapter 7 should show that redemption is not escape from creaturely continuity, not theatrical renaming, and not replacement of a damaged subject by a substitute, but the restorative reordering of the same wounded creaturely identity into truthful, life-giving coherence.

More briefly:

```text
Chapter 7 must show that redemption heals what corruption wounds.
```

## Chapter Function

The exact function of Chapter 7 is:

```text
to establish redemption as ontological restoration rather than mere legal fiction, symbolic relabelling, or discontinuous substitution
```

If this chapter fails, the upper arc will weaken in one of two ways:

- redemption will sound spiritually true but ontologically thin;
- or continuity will be preserved only by weakening the seriousness of corruption and rupture.

This chapter therefore carries the positive middle of the whole arc.

## Strongest Chapter Claim

The strongest current chapter claim is:

```text
Redemption restores the same damaged creaturely continuity into rightful order, so that healing is real because the wound is real and continuity is real, yet restoration does not collapse into mere continuation because corruption itself is genuinely judged, cleansed, and reordered.
```

This should be the chapter's governing claim.

## What the Chapter Must Accomplish

The chapter must accomplish five things.

## 1. Define Redemption Positively

The chapter must show that redemption is not simply:

- relief;
- pardon in abstraction;
- replacement;
- or escape.

It is:

```text
the healing of damaged continuity into rightful order
```

This must be stated clearly and early.

## 2. Preserve the Same Subject

The chapter must keep the same subject in view.

That means:

- the same creaturely continuity that was wounded;
- the same embodied subject that was burdened;
- the same reality that corruption fragmented.

Without this, redemption becomes substitution.

## 3. Preserve the Seriousness of Corruption

The chapter must not make restoration trivial by weakening corruption.

It must show that:

- corruption is real;
- damage is real;
- rupture is real;

and yet:

```text
restoration remains possible because continuity remains real
```

## 4. Distinguish Restoration from Rivals

The chapter should clearly distinguish redemption from:

- replacement
- escape
- counterfeit continuation

This is one of the chapter's main precision jobs.

## 5. Prepare the Reader for Resurrection

The chapter must leave the reader ready for the final question:

```text
how can the same creaturely identity be restored after real rupture without collapsing into substitution?
```

That is the handoff into the resurrection chapter.
