# AMS Score Recalibration Log v3

## Purpose

This document records the third formal recalibration round for the AMS substrate coherence programme.

Rounds 1 and 2 were driven mainly by:

- first `T1C` scaling
- extended `T1C` whole formation

Round 3 is driven by:

- boundary significance
- interface logic
- defect significance
- hysteresis
- retained structural history

This round therefore pressures the ontology in a stronger way than earlier rounds, because these are the cases where shallow explanatory systems often start to blur.

## Round 3 Principle

This round should favour cases that now demonstrate all or most of the following:

- coherent scaling into higher-order wholes
- meaningful boundary significance
- defect or interface sensitivity that still preserves whole identity
- retained history or path-dependent behaviour

Cases that remain weakly constrained or overly local should not be pushed upward simply because the overall programme has grown.
