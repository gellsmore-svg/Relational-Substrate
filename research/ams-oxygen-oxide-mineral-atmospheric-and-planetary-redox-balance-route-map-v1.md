## Purpose

This note integrates oxygen's `O7` and `O8` roles:

```text

```

The oxygen family entry proposed:

```text

```

This file completes the oxygen family by testing the final mechanism-facing claim: oxygen's polarity and oxidative power scale from local oxides and mineral lattices to atmospheric O2, combustion, photosynthetic oxygen evolution, and planetary redox balance.

The central claim is:

> Oxygen is the element that most clearly joins mineral closure, biological flux, and planetary history into one grammar. In solids it stabilises oxides and network lattices; in metabolism it is both produced and consumed; in the atmosphere it becomes a regime-setting oxidant reservoir; and at planetary scale it records the long contest between oxygenic production, reductant sinks, combustion and respiration, mineral burial, and redox rebalancing.

## Source Anchors

The source base supports these facts:

- Metal oxides are among the most abundant and stable materials in the Earth's crust, and for many metals oxides are the low-free-energy states in an oxidising environment.
- Oxide layers can passivate metals such as aluminium.
- Oxygenic photosynthesis in photosystem II splits water, producing electrons, protons, and molecular oxygen.
- The Mn4Ca oxygen-evolving complex in photosystem II is central to biological water oxidation and atmospheric oxygen production.
- Atmospheric oxygen accumulated during the Great Oxidation Event around 2.4-2.45 billion years ago, though its subsequent abundance fluctuated.
- Oxygen availability sets major energetic conditions for aerobic life and is linked to broader biogeochemical cycles.
- Combustion is an oxygen-consuming oxidation regime in which fuel architecture is driven toward more oxidised products with release of heat and radical chain chemistry.

### Oxygen and Carbon

Carbon is oxygen's main large-scale production/consumption partner.

```text
photosynthesis couples carbon fixation with oxygen release
respiration and combustion couple carbon oxidation with oxygen consumption
burial and sink competition influence net atmospheric oxygen
```

This is the major biospheric redox partnership in the current model.

### Oxygen and Phosphorus/Nitrogen

Nutrients regulate oxygen production indirectly by limiting biospheric productivity.

```text
phosphorus and nitrogen availability constrain oxygenic primary production
oxygen accumulation therefore depends partly on non-oxygen nutrient governance
```

This strengthens the cross-family ecology rather than weakening oxygen's identity.
